The Lateral Ventricle
A C-shaped cavity in each cerebral hemisphere, lined with ependyma. Has a central body + 3 horns (anterior, posterior, inferior).
⭐ High Yield
💧
CSF Volume
Total = 130 ml
30 ml in ventricles
100 ml in subarachnoid space
🎯 Exam Fav
🏭
CSF Formation
70% by choroid plexus (mainly lateral ventricle). Remainder from ependymal cells + pia mater cells.
🎯 Exam Fav
🌊
CSF Circulation
Lateral → Monro → 3rd → Sylvius → 4th → Magendie/Luschka → Subarachnoid → Arachnoid villi → Superior sagittal sinus
⭐ Must Know
⚖️
CSF Functions
① Protects brain/cord  ② Reduces weight 60× (1400→25 g)  ③ Substitutes CNS lymphatics  ④ Maintains chemical environment

Tapetum  ·  Optic radiation  ·  Inferior longitudinal fasciculus

Hydrocephalus
Abnormal ↑ CSF volume → ventricular dilation + ↑ head size.
Commonest cause = obstruction to circulation
🎯 Exam Fav
🧲
CSF Absorption
Main site = arachnoid villi & granulations → drain into dural sinuses, esp. superior sagittal sinus

🔴 Hydrocephalus — Causes & Mechanisms

🚧
Obstruction (Commonest)
Blockage anywhere in the CSF pathway. Most common site: Cerebral aqueduct of Sylvius.
Results in dilatation above the block.
🎯 #1 Cause
🧬
Overproduction (Rare)
Choroid plexus tumor → excess CSF exceeds absorption capacity → all ventricles dilate.
💡 Rare Cause
🩸
Inadequate Drainage
Thrombosis of superior sagittal sinus → arachnoid villi cannot drain CSF into venous blood → pressure builds.
💡 Clinical

📋 Key Clinical Features

👶
In Infants
Skull sutures not fused → head enlarges progressively. Sunset sign (eyes pushed down). Translucent scalp veins. Bulging fontanelle.
⭐ Classic Presentation
🧑
In Adults
Skull fused → raised ICP without head enlargement. Headache, vomiting, papilloedema, declining consciousness. Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH) triad: wet, wobbly, wacky.
💡 NPH Triad
Management
Principles of hydrocephalus treatment & CSF regulation
🚨 Emergency / Surgical
Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt — gold standard for chronic hydrocephalus
External ventricular drain (EVD) — acute ICP crisis
Endoscopic Third Ventriculostomy (ETV) — bypass aqueductal stenosis
Remove obstructing tumor or clot if identified
💊 Medical (Temporary)
Acetazolamide — reduces CSF production by inhibiting carbonic anhydrase
Furosemide — adjunct to reduce CSF formation
Osmotic agents (mannitol) — acute raised ICP
Steroids — reduce inflammation around obstruction
💚 CSF Regulation Principles
ICP regulated by adjusting production (↓) OR drainage (↑)
Normal ICP = 5–15 mmHg (supine)
Cerebral Perfusion Pressure = MAP − ICP (must keep > 60 mmHg)
Head elevation 30° reduces ICP by improving venous drainage
🔬 Underlying Cause
Choroid plexus tumor → surgical resection → stops overproduction
Superior sagittal sinus thrombosis → anticoagulation
Aqueductal stenosis → ETV preferred over shunt in children
Meningitis causing adhesions → treat infection + shunt
