🗺 Overview
Your complete visual summary of Basal Ganglia & Internal Capsule anatomy
⚠️

EXAM PEARL: Internal capsule stroke is the only lesion producing simultaneous motor + sensory + visual + auditory deficits — because all projection fibers condense into this tiny V-shaped band. Think "4-hit combo"!

BASAL GANGLIA — CORE CONCEPTS
🧬
Definition & Function
Group of nuclei deep in cerebral hemispheres. Part of the extrapyramidal motor system — inhibits unwanted motor activity to produce proper movement. No direct spinal cord connections.
HIGH YIELD
🗂
Anatomical Classification
Corpus Striatum (Caudate + Lentiform nucleus), Amygdaloid nucleus, and Claustrum. Subthalamic nucleus & Substantia Nigra are functionally related but anatomically distinct.
EXAM FAV
⚡
Functional Classification
Neostriatum (Striatum): Caudate + Putamen
Paleostriatum (Pallidum): Globus Pallidus
Regulation by dopamine from Substantia Nigra → Striatum.
CLINICAL PEARL
🏗
Corpus Striatum Shape
Caudate: C-shaped. Head = lateral wall of anterior horn LV. Body = floor of central part. Tail ends at amygdala.
Lentiform: Wedge-shaped. Putamen laterally, Globus Pallidus medially.
EXAM FAV
💉
Blood Supply — Corpus Striatum
Anterior part: Medial striate (ACA)
Main portion: Lateral striate (MCA) — "Arteries of cerebral haemorrhage"
Tail + Amygdala: Anterior choroidal artery (ICA)
HIGH YIELD
🔗
Claustrum Boundaries
Thin gray matter deep to insula.
Medially: External capsule (from lentiform)
Laterally: Extreme capsule (from insula)
Function: largely unknown.
CLINICAL PEARL
INTERNAL CAPSULE — CORE CONCEPTS
🔀
Structure
V-shaped band of projection fibers — the elevator going up & down between cortex and subcortical centers. Divided into: Anterior limb, Genu, Posterior limb (3 parts).
HIGH YIELD
🅰
Anterior Limb
Between lentiform nucleus and head of caudate.
Contains: Anterior thalamic radiation (thalamus → cingulate/prefrontal cortex) and Frontopontine fibers.
🫀
Genu
At the apex of the lentiform nucleus.
Contains ONLY: Corticobulbar fibers → to cranial nerve motor nuclei.
Lesion → contralateral cranial nerve motor deficits.
EXAM FAV
🅿
Posterior Limb — Lenticulothalamic
Between lentiform & thalamus.
Motor: Corticospinal, Frontorubral, Thalamocortical
Sensory: Main sensory thalamic radiation
Lesion → hemiplegia + hemianesthesia
HIGH YIELD
👁
Retrolenticular Part
Behind the lentiform nucleus.
Contains: Optic radiation (lateral geniculate → Area 17)
Lesion → Contralateral homonymous hemianopia
EXAM FAV
👂
Sublenticular Part
Below the lentiform nucleus.
Contains: Auditory radiation (medial geniculate → Area 41/42) + Nonfrontopontine fibers
Lesion → Bilateral diminution of hearing
⚠️

EXAM TIP: Case with combined motor + sensory + visual + hearing → immediately suspect Internal Capsule. It's the only site where all these fibers run together in a tiny space!

FLOWCHART — PARKINSON'S DISEASE MECHANISM
⚙️ FROM NIGRAL DEGENERATION → PARKINSON'S SIGNS
1
Substantia Nigra DegenerationDopamine-producing cells in S. nigra (pars compacta) progressively degenerate — etiology multifactorial.
2
Striatal Dopamine DepletionDopamine normally travels the nigrostriatal pathway to regulate striatum (caudate + putamen). Without it, inhibitory control is lost.
3
Loss of Extrapyramidal InhibitionThe basal ganglia can no longer properly inhibit unwanted motor activity. Excess motor output is unleashed.
4
Classic Parkinsonian Triad🤝 Resting "pill-rolling" tremor  |  Cogwheel rigidity  |  Bradykinesia. No paralysis, no sensory loss — extrapyramidal, not pyramidal!

DIAGNOSTIC ALGORITHM — SUSPECTED DEEP HEMISPHERE LESION
🔍 STEP-BY-STEP LOCALIZATION ALGORITHM
1
Does the patient have motor deficit?UMN hemiplegia (no wasting, hyperreflexia) → involves descending motor pathway.
2
Is there sensory loss on the same side as motor loss?If yes → lesion above the decussation; both corticospinal + sensory thalamic radiation affected simultaneously → suspect Internal Capsule.
3
Add visual field defect (hemianopia)?If yes → retrolenticular part (optic radiation) also involved → highly specific for IC.
4
Add bilateral hearing reduction?If yes → sublenticular part also affected → complete posterior limb infarct.
5
No paralysis / sensory loss, but movement disorder?Resting tremor + rigidity = Parkinson's (S. nigra). Flailing = Hemiballismus (STN). Jerky = Chorea (striatum).
6
Imaging: MRI/CT brainMRI superior for delineating deep nuclei and internal capsule lesions. CT adequate for acute hemorrhage from lateral striate artery rupture.
BLOOD SUPPLY — INVESTIGATION RELEVANCE
🩸
Lateral Striate Arteries (MCA)
Supply main corpus striatum (excluding anterior/tail). Called "arteries of cerebral haemorrhage" — spontaneous rupture = most common cause of IC stroke/basal ganglia bleed.
HIGH YIELD
🩸
Anterior Choroidal Artery (ICA)
Supplies tail of caudate + amygdala. Also supplies part of the IC. Stroke → can cause classic IC syndrome including hemianopia due to optic radiation involvement.
🩸
Medial Striate (ACA)
Supplies anterior part of corpus striatum (head of caudate, anterior putamen). Also called Heubner's artery.
💊 Treatment & Management
Disease-specific management for basal ganglia disorders and IC stroke
🚨 ACUTE — Internal Capsule Stroke
Acute Ischaemic Stroke
IV tPA (alteplase) within 4.5 hrs of symptom onset. Imaging to exclude haemorrhage first. Aspirin 300mg loading if thrombolysis not given.
Haemorrhagic Stroke
Reverse anticoagulation if applicable. Blood pressure control. Neurosurgical consult for significant haematoma volume causing mass effect.
Supportive Care
Airway / swallowing assessment (corticobulbar involvement). Physiotherapy for hemiplegia. Visual field rehabilitation for hemianopia.

ANATOMY EXAM NOTE: In this anatomy course, treatment detail is not the primary focus. Know the mechanism: dopamine depletion from S. nigra degeneration. Treatment (levodopa) restores dopamine. No surgery on IC itself — IC lesions from stroke are managed medically/rehab.

✅ SYMPTOMATIC — Hemiballismus
Dopamine Antagonists
Haloperidol or other antipsychotics reduce hemiballismus severity by antagonising dopamine in the basal ganglia circuit.
Tetrabenazine
Depletes presynaptic monoamines (dopamine). Used in severe, refractory hemiballismus and chorea.
Underlying Cause
Often post-stroke (subthalamic nucleus infarct) — manage stroke risk factors. Many cases spontaneously improve over weeks.
