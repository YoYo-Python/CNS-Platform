Brain Stem — Three Levels, One Master Region

The brain stem comprises the medulla oblongata, pons, and midbrain. It houses 10 of the 12 cranial nerve nuclei, major ascending/descending tracts, and life-critical cardiovascular and respiratory centers. Lesions produce highly characteristic crossed syndromes: ipsilateral cranial nerve deficit + contralateral long-tract signs.

📍 Structural Hierarchy
🧠
Most Rostral
Midbrain
CN III & IV exit. Contains crus cerebri, substantia nigra, red nucleus, tectum (superior/inferior colliculi). Key syndromes: Weber's, Benedikt's, Parinaud's.
🌉
Middle
Pons
CN V–VIII exit. Divided into basis pontis (ventral — pyramidal + pontine nuclei) and tegmentum (dorsal — lemnisci, CN nuclei). Facial colliculus = CN VI nucleus + CN VII fibers. ⚠️ EXAM FAVOURITE
🔻
Most Caudal
Medulla Oblongata
CN IX–XII exit. Medial zone: gracile/cuneate nuclei, pyramidal decussation, hypoglossal nucleus. Lateral zone: spinal trigeminal nucleus, nucleus ambiguus, inferior olivary complex, ICP.
⭐ High-Yield Structures
🟡
Substantia Nigra
Pars compacta: melanin-containing, secretes dopamine. Pars reticularis: secretes GABA. Degeneration of compacta → Parkinson's disease.
⚠️ EXAMHigh-Yield
🔵
Facial Colliculus
Located in floor of 4th ventricle (pons). Contains abducent nucleus surrounded by looping motor fibers of the facial nerve.
⚠️ EXAMClinical Pearl
🟢
Nucleus Ambiguus
Upper ⅓ → CN IX (stylopharyngeus). Middle ⅓ → CN X (pharynx/palate/cricothyroid). Lower ⅓ → CN XI cranial (larynx). Damage → hoarseness + dysphagia.
High-Yield
🟠
Nucleus Solitarius
Upper ⅓: taste via CN VII (ant 2/3 tongue), IX (post 1/3), X (epiglottis). Lower ⅔: general visceral sensation via IX & X.
High-Yield
🔴
Inferior Olivary Complex
Receives cortical/BG/red nucleus motor input + spinal sensory input. Sends olivocerebellar tract via ICP to cerebellum. Key for cerebellar movement control.
Pathway
🟣
Medial Lemniscus
Formed by sensory decussation of gracile & cuneate nuclei axons. Carries conscious proprioception, discriminative touch, vibration from contralateral body.
High-Yield
⚪
Accessory Cuneate Nucleus
Cerebellar relay → unconscious proprioception upper limb. Forms cuneocerebellar tract (dorsal external arcuate fibers) → ICP → cerebellum.
Clinical Pearl
🌀
Crus Cerebri
Medial ⅕: frontopontine fibers. Lateral ⅕: nonfrontopontine fibers. Middle ⅗: pyramidal tract (corticospinal + corticobulbar).
