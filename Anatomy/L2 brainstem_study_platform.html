🗺️ Topic Overview

5 major topics · 25 MCQs · 12 Viva questions · 6 Clinical cases

⚠️ High-Yield

CN IV — Unique Exit

The trochlear nerve (CN IV) is the ONLY cranial nerve exiting from the dorsal aspect of the brainstem, below the inferior colliculus. Classic MCQ trap!

🎯 Exam-Favorite

Facial Colliculus — The Trap

Formed by the abducens nucleus (CN VI) encircled by facial nerve fibers — NOT the facial nucleus. Lesion → lost eye abduction + facial palsy.

🎯 Exam-Favorite

Cerebellopontine Angle Contents

CN VII, CN VIII, lateral recess of 4th ventricle, flocculus of cerebellum, and the AICA. CPA mass → hearing loss + facial palsy + gag reflex loss.

💎 Clinical Pearl

Hydrocephalus Mechanism

The cerebral aqueduct of Sylvius is the narrowest point of CSF circulation. Its obstruction → upstream ventricle dilation → congenital hydrocephalus.

⚠️ High-Yield

Neural Crest Derivatives

DRG, sensory ganglia (CN V/VII/IX/X), autonomic ganglia, pia/arachnoid mater, Schwann cells, suprarenal medulla, melanocytes, odontoblasts. Classic viva topic!

💎 Clinical Pearl

Area Postrema (Obex)

The vomiting center, located at the inferior angle of the 4th ventricle floor (open medulla), near the vagal trigone. Lesion → intractable vomiting.

🧬 Embryology

Neuropore Closure Dates

Neural tube begins at week 3. Cranial neuropore closes day 25, caudal neuropore closes day 27. Failure → anencephaly / spina bifida.

⚠️ High-Yield

Locus Ceruleus vs Substantia Nigra

Locus ceruleus (dorsal pons) → secretes noradrenaline. Substantia nigra (midbrain peduncle) → secretes dopamine. Common confuse trap!

📅 Embryological Timeline

Week 3
Neural Plate Formation
Notochord induces dorsal ectoderm to thicken → Neural plate. Lateral margins elevate → Neural folds. Center depresses → Neural groove.
Week 4
Neural Tube Closes
Neural folds fuse → Neural tube separates from ectoderm. Neural crest cells migrate between tube and ectoderm.
Day 25
Cranial Neuropore Closes ⚠️
Anterior end seals. Failure → Anencephaly (skull vault absent, malformed brain exposed).
Day 27
Caudal Neuropore Closes ⚠️
Posterior end seals. Failure → Spina bifida (occulta / meningocele / meningomyelocele depending on severity).
Ongoing
Brain Vesicle Differentiation
Cranial end dilates → 3 primary vesicles → 5 secondary divisions. Prosencephalon → Telencephalon + Diencephalon. Rhombencephalon → Metencephalon + Myelencephalon.

💊 Clinical Management

Congenital anomalies · Brainstem lesion sequelae

🚨 Emergency

Congenital Hydrocephalus

  • Neurosurgical referral immediately
  • Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt insertion
  • Endoscopic 3rd ventriculostomy (ETV)
  • Monitor head circumference & ICP
  • Caused by aqueduct of Sylvius stenosis
🚨 Emergency

CPA Mass / Acoustic Neuroma

  • Microsurgical resection (gold standard)
  • Stereotactic radiosurgery (Gamma Knife)
  • Observation for small lesions
  • Preserve CN VII function priority
  • Preop audiometry + BAER testing
🔵 Surgical

Meningomyelocele

  • Early surgical closure within 24–48 hrs
  • Prevent infection / CSF leak
  • VP shunt for associated hydrocephalus
  • Multidisciplinary (neurosurgery, urology, ortho)
  • Folic acid supplementation in prevention
🔵 Surgical

Meningocele

  • Elective surgical repair
  • Excision of cyst + dural closure
  • Lower urgency than meningomyelocele
  • Neurological prognosis generally good
✅ Conservative

Spina Bifida Occulta

  • No treatment required
  • Incidental finding in ~10% population
  • Reassure the patient
  • Annual review if tethered cord suspected
✅ Prevention

Neural Tube Defect Prevention

  • Folic acid 400 µg/day pre-conception
  • Folic acid 5 mg/day (high risk)
  • Antenatal screening: AFP + ultrasound
  • Amniocentesis if AFP elevated
