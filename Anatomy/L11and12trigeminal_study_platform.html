Exam Power Facts

• Jaw deviates to the same side as mandibular nerve injury (unopposed contralateral pterygoids)
• Lingual nerve = "dangerous position" behind last molar during 3rd molar extraction
• Pterygopalatine fossa communications are a major exam focus
• Trigeminal is the LARGEST cranial nerve with 4 nuclei (3 sensory, 1 motor)

⚠️ Top 3 Exam Traps

1. Jaw deviation = SAME side as V3 injury (right nerve → jaw goes right)
2. Buccal nerve (V3) is sensory only — buccinator motor = facial nerve (VII)
3. Cavernous sinus lesion affects V1 + V2 but NOT V3 (mandibular exits directly via foramen ovale)
