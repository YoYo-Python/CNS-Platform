Cranial Nerves IX – XII
The last four cranial nerves — anatomy, pathways, and clinical lesions
🔍
CN IX

Glossopharyngeal Nerve

  • Exits: Post-olivary fissure → Jugular foramen
  • Motor: Nucleus ambiguus → Stylopharyngeus
  • Para: Inferior salivary nucleus → Parotid gland
  • Sensory: Posterior 1/3 tongue (taste + general)
  • Carotid sinus/body baroreceptor reflex
  • Lesion: ↓ Gag reflex (afferent), ↓ Taste post-1/3
CN X

Vagus Nerve

  • Exits: Post-olivary fissure → Jugular foramen
  • Motor: Nucleus ambiguus → Pharynx, larynx, palate
  • Para: Dorsal motor nucleus → Heart, lungs, GIT to left colic flexure
  • Recurrent laryngeal → All laryngeal muscles except cricothyroid
  • External laryngeal → Cricothyroid only
  • Lesion: Uvula → normal side; hoarseness; dysphagia
CN XI

Accessory Nerve

  • Cranial root: Nucleus ambiguus → Joins vagus → Pharynx/larynx
  • Spinal root: C1–C5/6 → Foramen magnum → Jugular foramen
  • SCM: Rotate face to opposite side
  • Trapezius: Elevate shoulder
  • Lesion: Cannot shrug; cannot rotate face
  • Irritation: Torticollis (SCM spasm)
CN XII

Hypoglossal Nerve

  • Exits: Pre-olivary fissure → Hypoglossal canal
  • Motor: All tongue muscles EXCEPT palatoglossus
  • C1 hitchhikers: Ansa cervicalis, thyrohyoid, geniohyoid
  • LMN: Tongue → same side + atrophy
  • UMN: Tongue → opposite side, no atrophy
  • Bilateral: Motionless tongue, anarthria

⚡ High-Yield Tags

⚠️ Jugular foramen: CN IX, X, XI ⚠️ Hypoglossal canal: CN XII ⚠️ Nucleus Ambiguus: shared by IX, X, XI ⚠️ LMN XII → ipsilateral deviation + atrophy ⚠️ UMN XII → contralateral deviation, no atrophy Motor: Stylopharyngeus = CN IX only Motor: Palatoglossus = CN X (not XII) Taste anterior 2/3 = CN VII Taste posterior 1/3 = CN IX Taste root/epiglottis = CN X Para CN IX → Inferior salivary nucleus → Parotid Para CN X → Dorsal motor nucleus → Thorax + Abdomen Right RLN → hooks subclavian artery Left RLN → hooks aortic arch Gag reflex: CN IX afferent, CN X efferent C1 hitchhikes CN XII → ansa cervicalis sup. root

👅 Tongue Deviation Logic

⬇️ LMN Lesion of CN XII (e.g., Right side)

  • Right genioglossus paralyzed → cannot push right
  • Left genioglossus active → pushes tongue forward + right
  • Tongue deviates to SAME (right) side
  • Atrophy + wrinkling on right side
  • Cause: nucleus / nerve damage

⬆️ UMN Lesion of CN XII (e.g., Right side)

  • Corticospinal tract damaged above nucleus
  • Tongue deviates to OPPOSITE (left) side
  • No atrophy — muscle intact, nerve intact
  • Spastic tongue possible
  • Cause: stroke, tumour above medulla
