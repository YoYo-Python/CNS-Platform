White Matter Fibers

3 types: Commissural (cross midline), Association (same hemisphere), Projection (to subcortex). Each with distinct tracts and clinical roles.

🧠

Corpus Callosum

4 parts: Rostrum, Genu, Body, Splenium. Forms all walls of the lateral ventricle. Supplied by anterior cerebral artery (+ PCA to splenium).

💛

Limbic System

Emotions, recent memory, olfaction. 8 key nuclei including Hippocampus, Amygdala, Mamillary body, Habenular, Septal, Thalamus, PFC, Limbic lobe.

🌊

Fornix Pathway

Alveus → Fimbria → Crus → Body → Anterior columns → Mamillary body + Septal area. Main hippocampal output tract.

⚡

Limbic Striae

Amygdala →[Stria terminalis]→ Septal area →[Stria medullaris thalami]→ Habenular nucleus. Classic "handoff" sequence.

🔦

Posterior Commissure

Connects pretectal nuclei + Edinger-Westphal nuclei (bilateral light reflex) + oculomotor nuclei (upward gaze). Pure ⚠️ EXAM favorite.

📍 Clinical Signs & High-Yield Facts
Callosal ApraxiaLeft hand can't execute verbal commands. CC severed → language from dominant L hemisphere can't reach R motor cortex. ⚠️ EXAM
Alzheimer's DiseaseExtensive hippocampal degeneration. Loss of short-term → long-term memory conversion. High-Yield
Amygdala LesionLoss of fear, anger, and aggressive behavior. Bilateral: hypersexual + hyperphagia (Klüver-Bucy–like). Pearl
Posterior Commissure LesionBilateral light reflex abolished + loss of upward gaze (Parinaud syndrome elements). ⚠️ EXAM
Superior Longitudinal FasciculusLong association fiber connecting ALL 4 lobes. Lesion affects language (arcuate component) → conduction aphasia. High-Yield
Internal CapsuleProjection fibers. Anterior limb: lentiform + caudate. Genu: apex of lentiform. Posterior limb: lentiform + thalamus. ⚠️ EXAM
TapetumBody + Splenium fibers forming lateral wall of POSTERIOR + INFERIOR horns of lateral ventricle. Pearl
Mamillothalamic TractMamillary body → Anterior thalamic nucleus → Cingulate gyrus. Part of Papez circuit. Pathway
UncusPrimary olfactory area. Amygdala = olfactory ASSOCIATION area (appetitive behavior). High-Yield
Hippocampal CommissureConnects the two fornices and the two hippocampi. Different from other commissures. Pearl
Anterior CommissureConnects the two TEMPORAL lobes. Also connects olfactory bulbs. ⚠️ EXAM
Dentate GyrusSmall gyrus on the MEDIAL side of the hippocampus. Part of hippocampal formation. Pearl

✅ DIAGNOSIS: Callosal Apraxia (Split-Brain Syndrome)
⚠️ Urgent Anatomical Knowledge (Exam = Life or Death Priority)

🆘 Posterior Commissure Lesion

  • Bilateral pupillary light reflex ABOLISHED
  • Upward gaze paralysis (Parinaud's)
  • Usually from pineal gland tumors compressing posterior commissure
  • Emergency: raised ICP if associated with hydrocephalus

🆘 Corpus Callosum Injury

  • Callosal apraxia — surgical or traumatic severance
  • Split-brain: language inaccessible to non-dominant hemisphere
  • Left hand cannot execute verbal commands
  • Classic neurosurgical complication of transcallosal approaches

⚡ Hippocampal Degeneration

  • Progressive anterograde amnesia (can't form new memories)
  • Alzheimer's: extensive hippocampal + entorhinal degeneration
  • Korsakoff's: bilateral mamillary body damage (alcohol)
  • Short-term → long-term memory conversion lost

⚡ Amygdala Bilateral Lesion

  • Loss of fear and aggression response
  • Bilateral damage: Klüver-Bucy-like syndrome
  • Hyperphagia + hypersexuality
  • Placid, fearless behavior

✅ Internal Capsule Stroke

  • Pure motor hemiplegia (posterior limb involvement)
  • Lenticulostriate arteries supply posterior limb
  • Contralateral hemiplegia (corticospinal tract)
  • Thalamic pain if thalamic radiations affected

📋 Diagnostic Approach

  • MRI brain: gold standard for white matter tracts (DTI)
  • Neuropsychological testing: laterality, memory, language
  • Wada test: dominant hemisphere identification
  • Clinical exam: left-hand apraxia → corpus callosum
