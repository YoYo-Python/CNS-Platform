🗺️ Topic Overview

This module covers the arterial and venous supply of the brain, the Circle of Willis, and major cerebrovascular pathologies including global ischemia, focal infarction, intracerebral hemorrhage, aneurysms, and vascular malformations. These topics are high-yield for written exams, clinical vivas, and applied anatomy questions.

⭐ High-Yield Exam Facts

→ "Red neurons" = acute ischemia hallmark (eosinophilic, no nucleus, RNA lost) — highly tested in written exams
→ Hypertension #1 cause of massive intracerebral hemorrhage → deep structures (basal ganglia, internal capsule)
→ MCA is most common target of cardiac emboli (direct continuation of ICA)
→ Berry aneurysms lack internal elastic lamina → spontaneous subarachnoid hemorrhage
→ Congo red stain + apple-green birefringence = Cerebral Amyloid Angiopathy (CAA)
→ AVM = "bag of worms" = most dangerous vascular malformation
→ ACA central branches → ventral half anterior limb + anterior genu of internal capsule
→ MCA central branches → dorsal anterior limb + posterior limb of internal capsule

🩸 Intracerebral Hemorrhage — Hypertension vs CAA

FeatureHypertensive HemorrhageCerebral Amyloid Angiopathy
DefinitionMassive bleed due to elevated systemic BPBleed due to amyloid-β deposition in vessel walls
MechanismHyaline arteriolosclerosis + Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysm ruptureWeakened vessels from abnormal β-amyloid glycoprotein deposits
Vessels AffectedDeep penetrating arteriolesSuperficial cerebral & meningeal vessels
LocationDeep structures: basal ganglia, internal capsule, deep white matterLobar (superficial cerebral cortex)
DiagnosisClinical + imaging; vessel histology shows hyaline changesCongo red stain → apple-green birefringence under polarized light
Clinical ImpactMost common cause of intracerebral hemorrhage; severe motor deficitsRecurrent lobar bleeds; associated with Alzheimer's

🧠 Global vs Focal Cerebral Ischemia

FeatureGlobal IschemiaFocal Ischemia (Infarction)
DefinitionWidespread brain hypoxiaLocalized tissue death from occluded vessel
MechanismSevere hypotension (shock, cardiac arrest)Embolus or thrombus occluding specific artery
PatternEntire brain affected; neurons most vulnerableSpecific vascular territory (e.g., MCA territory)
Gross findingsWide gyri, narrow sulci (swelling)Pale infarct or hemorrhagic infarct (reperfusion)
Most vulnerableHippocampal pyramidal cells, Purkinje cellsTerritory of occluded vessel
OutcomeDiffuse brain swelling → possible brain deathFocal neurological deficit based on territory

💉 Embolic vs Thrombotic Infarction

FeatureEmbolicThrombotic
OriginHeart (post-MI mural thrombus, endocarditis) or carotid atherosclerosisIn-situ atherosclerotic plaque at branch points
Common siteMCA (direct continuation of ICA)Carotid bifurcation, MCA origin, basilar ends
FrequencyMore commonLess common
PatternOften hemorrhagic (reperfusion)Usually pale infarct
OnsetSuddenOften preceded by TIAs
