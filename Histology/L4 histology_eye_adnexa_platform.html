Cornea vs. Sclera — Transparency Explained

✅ Why Cornea is TRANSPARENT

  • 💧 Continuous evaporation of water
  • 🩸 Avascular — no blood vessels
  • 🔢 Equal refractive indices of cells, fibers, matrix
  • 📐 Regular arrangement of collagen fibers (substantia propria)
  • 🧱 Regular epithelium cell arrangement

❌ Why Sclera is OPAQUE

  • 🌊 Great amount of water
  • 🩸 Heavily vascularized C.T.
  • 🔀 Unequal refractive indices
  • 🌀 Irregular arrangement of C.T. fibers
⚠️ EXAM KEY: Bowman's membrane cannot regenerate — injury → fibrous scar → permanent opacity.

Clinical Correlations & Pathologies

🔴 Corneal Pathologies

Corneal Opacity / Scar

Bowman's membrane destroyed → C.T. repair → fibrous scar → permanent opacity. Cannot regenerate.

Corneal Vascularization

Normally avascular. Pathological ingrowth of vessels → immediate loss of transparency.

🟡 Eyelid Pathologies

Chalazion

Obstruction of Meibomian gland (tarsal plate). Painless, deep, not associated with lash. Chronic granulomatous inflammation.

Stye (Hordeolum)

Infection of Gland of Moll (sweat) or Zeis (sebaceous). Acute, painful, at eyelash follicle.

🟢 Retinal Pathologies

Retinitis Pigmentosa

Failure of rod disc turnover → debris accumulation → progressive dim-light vision loss (night blindness).

Blood-Retinal Barrier Breakdown

Failure of junctional complexes between pigmented epithelial cells → toxic macromolecule entry → retinal damage.

Macular Degeneration

Bruch's membrane dysfunction → abnormal macromolecule leakage from choriocapillaries → cone/fovea damage.

🔵 Glaucoma

Mechanism: Obstruction of aqueous humor drainage via the Canal of Schlemm → ↑ intraocular pressure → optic nerve compression → vision loss.

Histological basis: The ciliary processes continuously secrete aqueous humor. Normal IOP depends on balance between production and Canal of Schlemm drainage.

Clinical pearl: Aqueous humor provides nutrients to avascular structures — cornea and lens. Any disruption affects these structures too.

🟣 Lacrimal Gland Disease

Dry Eye (Keratoconjunctivitis Sicca)

Reduced tear production from lacrimal gland (serous acini failure) → loss of lysozyme → bacterial susceptibility + corneal dryness.

Dacryocystitis

Infection of the lacrimal sac (lined by pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium with goblet cells).
