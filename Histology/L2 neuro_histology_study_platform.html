Overview

Everything you need to know about Cerebrum & Cerebellum Histology — at a glance.

🏗️

Cerebral Cortex

6 layers with 5 main nerve cell types plus neuroglial cells. Each layer has distinct cells and connectivity patterns.

Cerebrum High Yield
🌿

Cerebellar Cortex

3 layers — molecular, Purkinje, granular — with only ONE efferent fiber type: the Purkinje cell axon.

Cerebellum Exam Fave
⭐

Betz Cells

The largest pyramidal cells in the CNS. Located exclusively in Layer V (Internal Pyramidal Layer) of the motor cortex.

MCQ Target High Yield
📤

Only Cerebellar Efferent

Purkinje cell axons are the sole output of the cerebellar cortex → deep cerebellar nuclei + vestibular nuclei.

MCQ Target Clinical Pearl
🔄

Martinotti — "Inverted Cell"

Short dendrites + ascending axon ending in Layer I. The ONLY cell with an axon that travels towards the cortical surface.

Clinical Pearl Cerebrum
🌹

Cerebellar Rosette

Mossy fiber terminal + granule cell dendrites + Golgi cell axons = cerebellar glomerulus. A key histological landmark.

⭐These are the TOP 3 EXAM HIGHLIGHTS per the lecturer: (1) Betz cells in Layer V, (2) Purkinje axon = only cerebellar efferent, (3) Martinotti cell = "inverted cell" with ascending axon to Layer I.

Full Essay Answers 📚

Complete model answers for the 4 essay questions — ready for exam writing practice.

⭐ Essay Q1 — 6 Layers of the Cerebral Cortex

Layer I — Molecular: Contains Horizontal cells of Cajal. Small, fusiform, horizontally oriented. Axons run parallel to surface, synapse with pyramidal cell dendrites.
Layer II — External Granular: Contains stellate cells and small pyramidal cells.
Layer III — External Pyramidal: Contains medium-sized pyramidal cells and Martinotti cells.
Layer IV — Internal Granular: Contains numerous granule cells and Martinotti cells.
Layer V — Internal Pyramidal (Ganglionic): ⭐ Contains Betz cells (largest pyramidal cells of the motor cortex), few granule cells, and Martinotti cells. Origin of major descending motor tracts.
Layer VI — Multiform: Contains small pyramidal, granule, Martinotti, and fusiform cells.

📝 Essay Q2 — 5 Cell Types of Cerebral Cortex

Pyramidal cells: Pyramid-shaped. Apical dendrite → Molecular layer. Basal dendrites at same level. Axon enters white matter as projection/association/commissural fibers.
Stellate (Granule) cells: Small (4-8 µm), star-shaped. Short dendrites in all directions. Short local axon. Found in multiple layers.
Martinotti cells: Polygonal, the "inverted cell." Short dendrites + axon that uniquely ascends toward the surface to end in Layer I.
Fusiform cells: Spindle-shaped. Dendrites arise from both poles. Axon enters white matter.
Horizontal cells of Cajal: Small, fusiform, horizontally oriented. Exclusively in Layer I. Axon runs parallel to surface; synapses with pyramidal cell dendrites, receiving input from Martinotti axons.

🌿 Essay Q3 — 3 Layers of Cerebellar Cortex

Outer Molecular Layer: Mainly fibers, few cells. Superficial = Stellate cells. Deep = Basket cells (long axons form basket-like endings around Purkinje cell bodies). Receives: parallel fibers (from granule axons) and climbing fibers (from inferior olivary nucleus).
Middle Purkinje Cell Layer: Single row of large, pyriform Purkinje cells. Dendrites fan into molecular layer. Axons = ONLY efferent of cerebellar cortex, entering white matter → deep cerebellar + vestibular nuclei.
Inner Granular Layer: Closely packed granule cells (axons ascend → parallel fibers). Scattered Golgi cells (dendrites fan in multiple planes, axons synapse at rosettes).

🔀 Essay Q4 — Climbing vs Mossy Fibers

Climbing Fibers: Origin = Inferior olivary nucleus. Travel directly to the molecular layer, bypassing the granular layer. Terminate on the dendrites of Purkinje cells. Represent direct excitatory afferent input.
Mossy Fibers: Origin = Spinal cord or brain stem. Terminate in inner granular layer. End as rosette-like (mossy) terminals forming cerebellar glomeruli, synapsing with granule cell dendrites and Golgi cell axons. Indirect route to Purkinje cells via granule cells → parallel fibers.
