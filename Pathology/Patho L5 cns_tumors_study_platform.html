🧠 CNS Tumor Categories Core Concepts
⭐
Gliomas (Neuroepithelial)
Astrocytoma · Oligodendroglioma · Ependymoma
Most common primary CNS tumors. Astrocytoma is #1. Graded I–IV by progression. Located in cerebellum (children) or cerebrum (adults).
⚠️ EXAM FAVHIGH YIELD
🧒
Embryonal Tumors
Medulloblastoma
Children. "Small blue round cell tumor." High proliferation rate. 4th ventricle. Can spread trans-coelomically via subarachnoid space.
⚠️ EXAM FAV
🎗️
Meningioma
Meningothelial Tumor
From arachnoid villi endothelial cells. Dura-based. Whorls + Psammoma bodies. Estrogen-sensitive — grows in pregnancy. Benign but compresses brain.
⚠️ EXAM FAV💎 PEARL
🧵
Peripheral Nerve Tumors
Schwannoma · Neurofibroma
Schwannoma: capsulated, one side of nerve. Neurofibroma: uncapsulated, nerve passes through. NF1 (Von Recklinghausen) → malignant risk.
HIGH YIELD
🔴
Metastatic Tumors
Most common malignant brain tumor (30%)
Sources: Lung, breast, kidney. Multiple nodules at grey-white matter junction. Surrounded by edema. NOT a primary CNS tumor.
⚠️ EXAM FAV
📈
Raised ICP Effects
Universal Complication
Headache · Papilledema (retinal vein compression) · Hemiplegia · Brain herniation. Caused by tumor mass + edema + CSF obstruction.
💎 PEARL

🔑 Key Clinical Pearls
⚠️ EXAM
Psammoma bodies + dura-based tumor = Meningioma
⚠️ EXAM
Capsulated on one side of nerve = Schwannoma; nerve passes through = Neurofibroma
⚠️ EXAM
Small blue round cell tumor in child's 4th ventricle = Medulloblastoma
💎 PEARL
Grade IV distinguished by necrosis + glomeruloid vascular proliferation (Grade III has mitosis without necrosis)
💎 PEARL
Cafe au lait spots = Von Recklinghausen disease = risk of malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor
HIGH YIELD
Most common malignant brain tumor overall = Metastatic (not astrocytoma). Common sources: lung, breast, kidney
