📋 Overview — Neurodegenerative Diseases

Quick summary of Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and Huntington's disease. Every fact you need at a glance.

🧠 Dementia — Definition

Development of memory impairment and other cognitive deficits with preservation of normal level of consciousness. It is a progressive loss of intellectual capabilities without affecting consciousness.

HIGH-YIELDEXAM FAVORITE

🟥 Alzheimer Disease (AD)

Most common cause of dementia in the elderly. Characterized by extracellular Amyloid β plaques and intracellular hyperphosphorylated tau tangles. Causes cortical atrophy with widened sulci and compensatory ventricular enlargement (hydrocephalus ex vacuo). Usually begins after age 50. 5–10% familial.

HIGH-YIELD⚠️ EXAM

🟩 Parkinson Disease (PD)

Loss of pigmented catecholaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra & locus ceruleus. Accumulation of α-synuclein Lewy bodies. Causes bradykinesia, rigidity, pill-rolling tremor, festinating gait, and masked facies. Men > Women.

HIGH-YIELDCLINICAL PEARL

🟨 Huntington Disease (HD)

Familial genetic disease with CAG trinucleotide repeats. Loss of GABA (main inhibitory neurotransmitter) from basal ganglia (corpus striatum). Leads to chorea (jerky involuntary movements), progressive motor loss, and dementia. Fatal course.

⏳ Disease Feature Timeline

Comparative progression of key neurodegenerative diseases

🟢 Normal State

Extracellular proteins are metabolized by alpha-secretase (normal pathway). Intracellular tau proteins stabilize microtubules. Dopaminergic neurons produce adequate dopamine. GABA maintains inhibitory braking in basal ganglia.

🟡 Early Pathological Changes

AD: Beta-secretase starts abnormally cleaving protein → Amyloid β forms. Tau becomes hyperphosphorylated. PD: α-synuclein misfolds, proteasome degradation fails. HD: CAG repeats expand, GABA neurons begin to degenerate.

🟠 Accumulation Phase

AD: Plaques accumulate extracellularly; tangles accumulate intracellularly → neuronal toxicity and death → cortical atrophy. PD: Lewy bodies form → substantia nigra pallor. HD: Corpus striatum atrophies → GABA loss.

🔴 Clinical Manifestation

AD: Progressive dementia with memory loss, brain atrophy, ventricle enlargement. PD: Bradykinesia, rigidity, pill-rolling tremor, shuffling gait, mask-like face. HD: Chorea, progressive motor loss, dementia, death.

🔬 Pathophysiology

Interactive exploration of disease mechanisms

🟥 Alzheimer Disease — Pathophysiology Flow

Normal extracellular protein cleaved by alpha-secretase (normal pathway)
⬇️
Abnormal metabolism by beta-secretase → forms toxic Amyloid β peptides
⬇️
Amyloid β aggregates extracellularly → forms Senile (Neuritic) Plaques
⬇️ (Simultaneously)
Intracellular microtubule tau protein becomes hyperphosphorylated
⬇️
Hyperphosphorylated tau forms intracellular Neurofibrillary Tangles (NFTs)
⬇️
Plaques + Tangles → Neuronal toxicity & death
⬇️
Cortical atrophy → widening of sulci → compensatory ventricular enlargement (Hydrocephalus Ex Vacuo)
⬇️
Progressive dementia with preserved consciousness

🟩 Parkinson Disease — Pathophysiology Flow

Normal α-synuclein protein is properly folded and degraded by proteasome
⬇️
α-synuclein misfolds → defective proteasome degradation
⬇️
Misfolded α-synuclein aggregates → forms intracytoplasmic eosinophilic inclusions (Lewy Bodies)
⬇️
Death of pigmented catecholaminergic neurons in Substantia Nigra & Locus Ceruleus
⬇️
Macroscopic: Pallor of substantia nigra (loss of pigmentation)
⬇️
Loss of dopamine production → disrupted basal ganglia regulation → loss of motor "brakes"
⬇️
Clinical: Bradykinesia, rigidity, pill-rolling tremor, festinating gait, masked facies

🟨 Huntington Disease — Pathophysiology Flow

Autosomal dominant genetic mutation: expansion of CAG trinucleotide repeats
⬇️
Neurotoxicity in basal ganglia (corpus striatum)
⬇️
Loss of GABAergic neurons → depletion of GABA (main inhibitory neurotransmitter)
⬇️
Atrophy of the basal ganglia
⬇️
Loss of inhibitory "brakes" in the brain
⬇️
Clinical: Chorea (jerky involuntary movements) + progressive motor loss + dementia → fatal

🔀 Diagnostic Algorithm — Approach to Dementia

Patient presents with progressive cognitive decline + preserved consciousness
⬇️
Is there significant motor involvement?
⬇️ Yes ⬇️ No (primarily cognitive)
Motor: Tremor, Rigidity, Bradykinesia → Parkinson's

Motor: Chorea, jerky movements → Huntington's
Check imaging: Cortical atrophy + ventricular enlargement → Alzheimer's

Spongy degeneration → Prion disease

🔬 Key Investigation Findings

🟥 Alzheimer's

Gross: Cortical atrophy, widened sulci, hydrocephalus ex vacuo
Micro: Extracellular amyloid plaques, intracellular NFTs
Stain: Silver stain, Congo red (amyloid)

🟩 Parkinson's

Gross: Pallor of substantia nigra & locus ceruleus
Micro: Lewy bodies (eosinophilic intracytoplasmic inclusions with α-synuclein)
Clinical: Diagnosis is primarily clinical

🟨 Huntington's

Gross: Atrophy of basal ganglia (corpus striatum)
Genetic: CAG trinucleotide repeat expansion testing
Clinical: Chorea + family history (autosomal dominant)

💊 Treatment

Pharmacological management of neurodegenerative diseases

🟥 Alzheimer Disease Treatment

  • 💊
    Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors (e.g., Rivastigmine, Donepezil) — Slow degradation of ACh, improve neuropsychiatric symptoms like apathy. Symptomatic only.
  • 💊
    NMDA Receptor Antagonists (Memantine) — Block glutamate excitotoxicity, slow cognitive decline. Used in moderate-severe AD.
  • 💉
    Monoclonal Antibodies against Amyloid β — Newer disease-modifying therapies targeting amyloid accumulation.
  • ⚠️
    No cure exists. All treatments aim to slow progression or manage symptoms.

🟩 Parkinson Disease Treatment

  • 💊
    L-Dopa (Levodopa) — Dopamine precursor; crosses BBB and is converted to dopamine. Gold standard treatment.
  • 💊
    Carbidopa — Peripheral dopa decarboxylase inhibitor; given with L-dopa to prevent peripheral conversion and reduce side effects.
  • 💊
    Dopamine Agonists — Directly stimulate dopamine receptors (e.g., Pramipexole).
  • 💊
    MAO-B Inhibitors — Prevent dopamine breakdown (e.g., Selegiline).
  • ⚠️
    Treatment is symptomatic; disease continues to progress.

🟨 Huntington Disease Treatment

  • 💊
    Tetrabenazine — Reduces chorea by depleting dopamine from nerve terminals.
  • 🩺
    Supportive care — Physical therapy, speech therapy, nutritional support.
  • ⚠️
    No disease-modifying therapy. Course is progressive and fatal.
  • 🧬
    Genetic counseling — Essential for family members due to autosomal dominant inheritance.

🔵 Other Causes of Dementia

  • 💊
    Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome — Caused by Thiamine (B1) deficiency. Treat with IV thiamine.
  • 🦠
    Prion Disease (CJD/Mad Cow) — "Spongy degeneration" of brain. No effective treatment.
  • 🦠
    HIV-associated Dementia — Manage with antiretroviral therapy.
  • 🩺
    Multi-infarct (Vascular) Dementia — Control vascular risk factors.
