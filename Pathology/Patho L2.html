🧬 CNS Infections — High-Yield Overview

The central nervous system is protected by the blood-brain barrier, yet pathogens breach it via three main routes. This lecture covers bacterial meningitis (age-specific organisms!), brain abscess, viral encephalitis including Rabies and Polio, and the unique world of prion diseases.

Routes of CNS Infection
🩸

Hematogenous Spread

Via arterial circulation or retrograde venous spread from veins of the face. Most common route for bacteria. Causes meningitis and encephalitis.

🔪

Direct Spread / Implantation

Trauma, iatrogenic (lumbar puncture), adjacent structures (sinuses, middle ear, teeth, vertebrae), or congenital defects (meningomyelocele).

🧠

Nerve Spread

Retrograde axonal transport along peripheral nerves — bypasses blood-brain barrier. Key examples: Rabies Herpes Zoster

Key Clinical Concepts
🧫

Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome

Meningococcal septicemia → massive toxin release → bilateral adrenal hemorrhage → fatal shock

⚠️ EXAM FATAL
🧪

Brain Abscess vs Meningitis

Both ↑ neutrophils & protein in CSF. KEY DIFFERENCE: Brain abscess has normal glucose (bacteria not in CSF)

💡 Clinical Pearl
🦠

PML (Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy)

JC polyomavirus → destroys oligodendrocytes → white matter demyelination. Common in AIDS/immunocompromised

HIGH YIELD
🐺

Negri Bodies in Rabies

Eosinophilic intracytoplasmic viral RNA inclusions. Found in hippocampal pyramidal cells and cerebellar Purkinje cells

⚠️ EXAM
🧟

Prion Diseases

Misfolded protein (PrP) — heat and acid resistant. Examples: CJD, Mad Cow Disease. No DNA/RNA involved

UNIQUE MECHANISM
🦷

Polio — AHC Destruction

Fecal-oral → viremia → anterior horn cells → permanent flaccid paralysis. Post-polio syndrome 15-40 yrs later

💡 Motor Only

Diagnostic Algorithm — Suspected Meningitis
1

History & Clinical Features

Fever, headache, neck stiffness, photophobia. Check Kernig's sign and Brudzinski's sign (meningeal irritation). Note age of patient!

2

Urgency Assessment

Look for papilledema or focal neuro deficits — if present, CT FIRST before LP to rule out raised ICP and herniation risk

3

Lumbar Puncture (CSF Analysis)

Cell count, glucose, protein, culture. Key differentiator: Glucose level — low = bacterial; normal = viral or abscess

4

Imaging (MRI/CT)

For abscess: ring-enhancing lesion. HSV-1: temporal lobe changes. PML: multifocal white matter lesions

5

Culture / PCR / Serology

Blood cultures (bacteria), viral PCR (HSV, enteroviruses), specific antibody testing as indicated by suspected organism

🚫 Prion Diseases — No Cure

Prion diseases (CJD, Mad Cow) have no effective treatment. They are uniformly fatal. The misfolded PrP protein resists heat, acid, and standard sterilization methods. Prevention focuses on: avoiding infected meat, using proper surgical instrument sterilization, and screening blood/organ donors.
