⚡High-Yield Facts
Neural Tube

Closes by 3rd gestational week. Failure → NTDs

Spina Bifida Occulta

Bony lumbo-sacral defect + tuft of hair. No herniation.

Holoprosencephaly

Severest form → cyclopia (single midline eye)

Arnold-Chiari Triad

Small fossa + myelomeningocele + obstructive hydrocephalus

Dandy-Walker

Absent cerebellar vermis → 4th ventricle cyst dilation

Hydro — Newborn

Open sutures → skull enlargement (head circumference ↑)

Hydro — Adult

Fused skull → dementia + wide gait + incontinence

Ex Vacuo

Alzheimer's + Tau protein misfolding → atrophy → compensatory CSF dilation

Most Common NCC Cause (Newborn)

Stricture of Aqueduct of Sylvius

Absent Corpus Callosum

Rare; exclusively affects females. Seizures + retinal abnormalities.

Polymicrogyria

Excessive folding → multiple small gyri → thick cortex

Communicating Hydrocephalus

Choroid plexus papilloma (↑production) OR postmeningitic arachnoid granulation scarring (↓absorption)

🔄Pathogenesis of Obstructive Hydrocephalus
1
Stricture / Tumor — Aqueduct of Sylvius or 4th ventricle mass (e.g. ependymoma)
2
Internal blockage — CSF produced by choroid plexus cannot reach subarachnoid space
3
CSF backs up — Pressure builds behind the obstruction
4
Ventricular dilation — Lateral and 3rd ventricles expand
5A
Newborn: Unfused sutures → skull expands → enlarged head circumference
5B
Adult: Fused skull → brain compressed → dementia, gait, incontinence, death if untreated

🔄Hydrocephalus Ex Vacuo — Alzheimer's Model
1
Tau protein misfolding — Aberrant tau accumulates in neurons (Alzheimer's disease)
2
Cellular atrophy — Neurons die; brain mass decreases progressively
3
Excess cranial space — Ventricles and sulci appear to enlarge on imaging
4
Compensatory CSF fill — Fluid passively fills vacated space
5
Normal CSF pressure — This is NOT true hydrocephalus; no obstruction or overproduction

💊Management Principles
🚨 Urgent / Surgical Interventions
🔧
VP Shunt (Ventriculoperitoneal)
Primary treatment for obstructive & communicating hydrocephalus. Drains CSF from ventricles to peritoneum.
🏥
Surgical Repair of Myelomeningocele
Closure within 24–72h of birth to prevent infection and minimize further neurological injury.
🧠
Endoscopic Third Ventriculostomy (ETV)
Alternative to shunt for non-communicating hydrocephalus. Creates bypass for CSF flow.
⚙️
Posterior Fossa Decompression (Arnold-Chiari)
Suboccipital craniectomy to enlarge foramen magnum and relieve brainstem compression.
⚠️ Preventive & Prenatal Measures
💊
Folic Acid Supplementation
400–800 mcg/day pre-conception and in first trimester significantly reduces NTD risk.
🔬
TORCH Screening in Pregnancy
Toxoplasmosis, Rubella, CMV, Syphilis — 1st trimester infections most dangerous for fetal brain development.
📡
Prenatal Ultrasound / AFP Screening
Elevated maternal serum AFP → open NTD. Ultrasound for structural CNS anomalies from 18–20 weeks.
✅ Supportive & Symptomatic Management
💧
Hydrocephalus Ex Vacuo
No shunting needed. Manage the underlying cause (e.g., Alzheimer's dementia medications). No elevated ICP.
🧪
Acetazolamide (Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitor)
Temporarily reduces CSF production. Adjunct while awaiting definitive surgical treatment.
🏋️
Physiotherapy & Neurorehabilitation
Critical for myelomeningocele patients — bladder training, physiotherapy, orthotics for lower limb deficits.
👁️
Multidisciplinary Follow-up
Neurosurgery, neurology, ophthalmology (absent CC), urology. Monitor for shunt complications.
