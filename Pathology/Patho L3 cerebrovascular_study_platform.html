🩸 Types of Intracranial Hemorrhage
🔴
⚠️ High Yield
Extradural (Epidural) Hemorrhage
Arterial blood between skull and dura mater.
Mechanism: Temporal/parietal skull fracture → tear of middle meningeal artery → rapid arterial bleed → ↑ICP → brain herniation.
Arterial bloodEmergencyLucid interval
🟠
⚠️ Exam Favorite
Subdural Hemorrhage
Venous blood between dura and arachnoid mater.
Mechanism: Trauma → rupture of bridging cerebral veins. Elderly/alcoholics at higher risk due to cerebral atrophy stretching veins.
Venous bloodAcute / Chronic
🟡
Subarachnoid Hemorrhage
Blood between arachnoid and pia mater.
Mechanism: Rupture of berry aneurysms or AVMs → sudden severe headache + LOC + meningeal irritation.
Thunderclap HAAneurysm rupture
🩸
⚠️ High Yield
Intracerebral (Intraparenchymal) Hemorrhage
Blood escaping into brain tissue itself.
Most common cause: Systemic hypertension → rupture of MCA branches → deep structures (basal ganglia, thalamus, pons).
HTN #1 causeDeep structures

⚠️ Written Q
Global Cerebral Ischemia
Widespread ischemic injury from severe systemic hypotension (systolic <50 mmHg).
Most vulnerable: Pyramidal cells (hippocampus, neocortex) + Purkinje cells (cerebellum).
Red neuronsBrain death risk
🎯
Focal Cerebral Ischemia
Arterial or venous occlusion causing focal infarction (stroke).
Embolic: cardiac mural thrombi → MCA (most common). Thrombotic: atherosclerosis at carotid bifurcation.
MCA territoryCollaterals protect
💢
⚠️ Famous Q
Hypertensive Cerebrovascular Disease
Multiple brain changes from chronic high BP.
Lesions: Hyaline arteriolosclerosis → Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysms → lacunar infarcts → slit hemorrhages → massive hemorrhage.
Charcot-BouchardLacunar infarcts
🧬
Cerebral Amyloid Angiopathy
Aβ peptide deposition in vessel walls → lobar hemorrhages.
Diagnosis: Congo red stain → apple-green birefringence under polarized light. Causes dementia + lobar (cortical) hemorrhages.
Congo redLobar bleed
🫧
Berry (Saccular) Aneurysm
Congenital weakness of internal elastic lamina at arterial bifurcations.
Site: Circle of Willis bifurcations. Rupture → subarachnoid hemorrhage ("worst headache of life").
CongenitalSAH on rupture
🪱
Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM)
Tangled "bag of worms" vascular network.
Risk: Most dangerous vascular malformation. Mixed intraparenchymal + subarachnoid bleeding risk.
Highest riskBag of worms
🥊
Concussion
Reversible transient brain dysfunction — metabolic, no structural change.
Repeated concussions: → cumulative damage → neurodegenerative disorders (Parkinsonism). Seen in boxers.
ReversibleCTE risk
🧠
Contusion & Laceration
Contusion = brain bruise (intact surface). Laceration = gross tearing (penetrating trauma).
Contusion: Interstitial hemorrhage of gyri. Heals with hemosiderin staining. Coup/Contrecoup pattern.
Blunt traumaPenetrating = laceration

🩺 Clinical Presentation Clues
●Lucid interval → rapid deterioration: Extradural hemorrhage
●Worst headache of life (thunderclap): SAH / berry aneurysm rupture
●Elderly + minor trauma + confusion weeks later: Chronic subdural
●HTN + sudden focal deficit + deep bleed: Hypertensive intracerebral
●Post-cardiac arrest + diffuse deficit: Global ischemia
●Lobar bleed + dementia + elderly: Cerebral amyloid angiopathy

🚨 Neurosurgical Emergencies

Extradural hemorrhage: Surgical drainage STAT — arterial bleed causes rapid herniation
Acute subdural hematoma: Surgical decompression for acute ↑ICP
Berry aneurysm rupture → SAH: Neurological ICU, prevent rebleed, manage vasospasm
Hypertensive encephalopathy: Controlled BP reduction (diastolic >130 = trigger)
🎯
Condition-Specific Management
Chronic subdural: Drainage (burr hole) if symptomatic; monitor in mild cases
Global cerebral ischemia: Treat underlying hypotension; targeted temperature management post-arrest
Focal ischemic stroke: tPA within window (thrombotic/embolic); antiplatelet therapy
Concussion: Rest, cognitive & physical; avoid repeat trauma
CAA: No specific treatment; manage BP, avoid anticoagulants
🛡️
Prevention & Risk Reduction
Hypertension control: Most impactful — prevents intracerebral bleed, lacunar infarcts, Charcot-Bouchard rupture
Anticoagulation monitoring: Prevents embolic stroke but increases hemorrhagic risk in CAA
Concussion protocol: Graduated return-to-play; critical to avoid cumulative CTE
Aneurysm surveillance: Unruptured berry aneurysms monitored; elective clipping/coiling considered
