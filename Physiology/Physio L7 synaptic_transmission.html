📚 Overview — Synaptic Transmission
5 major topics · 20+ key concepts · USMLE-style prep

⚠️ EXAM CRITICAL — High-Yield Facts

G-Protein Coupled Receptors cause STRUCTURAL changes (via gene transcription/protein synthesis) — NOT just electrical changes. Metabotropic ≠ immediate ion flux. Memorize this!
Axon Hillock = site of Action Potential generation due to the highest density of voltage-gated Na⁺ channels in the neuron.
EPSPs have variable amplitudes (~0.5 mV) and MUST be summated (spatial or temporal) to reach threshold — unlike APs which follow the all-or-none law.
Synaptic Fatigue is a PROTECTIVE mechanism (e.g., terminates epileptic fits) caused by depletion of NT stores during rapid stimulation.
Na⁺ influx is ~7.5× greater than K⁺ efflux through cation channels — this net inward current produces the EPSP depolarization.

🔌 Topic 1 — Synapse Types

Electrical (gap junctions/connexons): bidirectional, fast, fatigue-resistant. Chemical: unidirectional, slower, fatigable — represents almost ALL CNS synapses.

High-YieldConnexons

⚗️ Topic 2 — Transmission Mechanism

AP → Ca²⁺ influx → vesicle exocytosis → NT diffuses cleft → ionotropic (fast) or metabotropic (slow, structural) receptor activation.

Exam ⚠️G-Protein

⚡ Topic 3 — PSPs & Summation

EPSP = cation channel → depolarization. IPSP = anion/K⁺ channel → hyperpolarization. Spatial + temporal summation required at axon hillock.

Exam ⚠️Pearl

🚫 Topic 4 — Inhibition

Presynaptic: GABA opens Cl⁻ channels on excitatory terminal → ↓AP amplitude → ↓Ca²⁺ → ↓NT release. Postsynaptic: direct hyperpolarization. Recurrent: feedback loop.

High-YieldGABA

🕐 Topic 5 — Synaptic Properties

Delay (0.5 msec), Fatigue (NT depletion, protective), Post-tetanic facilitation (presynaptic Ca²⁺), LTP (postsynaptic Ca²⁺, days, memory). pH: alkalosis → convulsions; acidosis → coma.

High-YieldMemory
