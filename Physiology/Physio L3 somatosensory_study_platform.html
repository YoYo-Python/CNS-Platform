Somatosensory Physiology

Thermoceptive sensations · Somatic pathways · Somatosensory cortex — Mansoura University

🌡️ Topic Overview

Somatic sensations arise from the skin, skeletal muscle, joints, and periosteum. Thermal sensation is the conscious awareness produced by cutaneous thermoreceptors (warm & cold). Signals travel via the Anterolateral (spinothalamic) system, relay in the thalamus (the "gate"), and project to the primary somatosensory cortex where they are perceived, localized, and integrated.

📍 Topic Progression

Topic 1 — Thermoreceptors

Cold (A-δ, 10–35°C, max 24°C) vs Warm (C fibers, 25–45°C, max 37°C) receptors. TRP channel mechanism. Paradoxical cold at 45°C. Anesthesia at 0°C.

Topic 2 — Somatic Sensory Pathways

Anterolateral System (ALS): DRG → Dorsal Horn → decussate via Anterior White Commissure → VPLN (Thalamus) → SI Cortex. Trigeminal variant for the head.

Topic 3 — Somatosensory Cortex

SI (Areas 3,1,2) post-central gyrus. SII (Area 40). Association Cortex (Areas 5,7). Homunculus. Lesion syndromes: Astereognosis, Parietal Neglect.

⚡ High-Yield Clinical Signs

❄️ Paradoxical Cold Sensation

Occurs at 45°C — cold receptors fire a brisk burst despite high temp. Cold receptor A-δ fibers excited paradoxically.

⚠️ ExamHigh-yield

🧊 Anesthesia at 0°C

Halts Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump → prevents repolarization → blocks all action potentials. Basis for cold local anesthesia.

⚠️ ExamClinical Pearl

🌡️ TRP Channel Cascade

Temp changes → ↑ metabolite concentration → TRP channel activation → Ca²⁺ & Na⁺ influx → receptor potential → AP.

🎯 Thalamus as Gate

"All somatic sensations must pass through the thalamus to reach the cortex" — VPLN for body, PMVN for head (trigeminal).

⚠️ Exam

🧠 Parietal Lobe Neglect

Lesion in Areas 5 & 7 → patient ignores contralateral body half. Also called Amorphosynthesis. Cannot coordinate motor feedback.

⚠️ ExamHigh-yield

🖐️ Astereognosis

Inability to recognize shape/form/texture by touch. Lesion in SI (Areas 3,1,2) or Association Cortex (5,7). Test: blindfold + key.

Clinical PearlHigh-yield

🔁 Phasic → Tonic Firing

Thermoreceptors show rapid phasic burst on stimulus onset, then settle to a slow tonic discharge. They are slowly adapting.

📍 Decussation Level

ALS crosses at the spinal cord level (anterior white commissure). DCML crosses in the medulla. Always distinguish!

💊 Applied Physiology & Concepts

Clinical applications of somatosensory physiology

🚨 Critical Mechanisms Exam-critical

Cold Anesthesia (0°C)

Arrests Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump → no repolarization → no AP generation. Blocks all sensory and motor transmission. Surgical application: tissue cooling before incision.

Paradoxical Cold at 45°C

Cold A-δ fibers paradoxically discharge at 45°C. Clinical implication: patients may misinterpret a warm stimulus as cold initially. Hot water = cold flash sensation at first.

TRPV1 Pharmacology

TRPV1 activated >43°C and by capsaicin. Capsaicin desensitizes TRPV1 → topical analgesic action. Basis for capsaicin pain creams.

🧠 Cortical Lesion Syndromes Clinically important

Parietal Lobe Neglect

Lesion: Areas 5 & 7 (right hemisphere dominant). Result: Patient ignores left body half — doesn't dress left arm, doesn't acknowledge left-sided stimuli. Also called Amorphosynthesis.

Astereognosis

Lesion: SI (Areas 3,1,2) or Association Cortex. Test: Place key in patient's hand with eyes closed — cannot identify object. Fine touch still present but complex recognition lost.

Tactile Learning Defect

Lesion: SII (Area 40). Result: Cannot form tactile memories. Cannot learn new object recognition by touch. Direct input from VPLN and SI needed for meaning assignment.

⚗️ Receptor Physiology Pearls Physiology

Why Glasses Are "Forgotten"

Touch receptors for glasses are rapidly adapting → stop firing quickly. But thermoreceptors are slowly adapting → maintain tonic discharge for hot cup. Key distinguishing principle.

Temperature Grading by Brain

Brain discriminates temperature grades by evaluating the relative ratio of discharge from cold vs warm receptors simultaneously. Neither alone is sufficient for fine grading.

Sensory Feedback → Motor Control

Areas 5 & 7 transmit pre-analyzed kinesthetic data to motor cortex. Without this feedback loop, movement becomes uncoordinated (ataxia-like). The sensory system directly drives accurate motor output.

📍 Pathway Decussation — Clinical Application Concept

Brown-Séquard Physiology Basis

ALS crosses at cord level → pain/temp loss contralateral to spinal lesion. DCML crosses at medulla → fine touch loss ipsilateral to spinal lesion. Dissociated sensory loss = spinal cord pathology.

Cortical Representation

Left cortex → right body (contralateral). Cortical area ∝ receptor density, not physical size. Hands + lips = large cortical area. Trunk = small cortical area. This is the Homunculus principle.
