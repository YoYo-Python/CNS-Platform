Clinical Features (Triad)

🫨 Static Tremors MCQ

  • Occur at rest
  • Pill-rolling pattern (thumb + index)
  • Disappear during movement
  • Increased by emotional excitement

🐌 Akinesia / Bradykinesia

  • Shuffling gait (short steps)
  • Mask-like face
  • Monotonous voice
  • Loss of automatic movements

💪 Rigidity

  • Lead-pipe rigidity
  • Cogwheel rigidity
  • Flexors > Extensors
  • "Gorilla attitude" (flexion posture)

⚡ Hemiballismus
Movement Type Violent, involuntary flinging movements of large body areas
Lesion Location Subthalamic Nucleus (STN)
Mechanism Loss of STN → ↓ excitation of GPi → Thalamus disinhibited
Common Cause Vascular lesions (stroke)
