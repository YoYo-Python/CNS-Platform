🧠 Somatic Motor System Overview

The Neural Command Hierarchy

Voluntary movements are orchestrated by Upper Motor Neurons (UMN) in the cortex/brainstem → relayed via descending tracts → to Lower Motor Neurons (LMN) in the spinal anterior horn cells → skeletal muscle. Two main highway systems: Pyramidal (voluntary skilled) and Extrapyramidal (posture, involuntary background).

🎯 Cortical Motor Areas
AREA 4 · PRIMARY MOTOR

Precentral Gyrus

Controls initiation of voluntary movements on contralateral, inverted side. Facilitates distal limb muscle tone. Large cortical area = fine movement (hand/tongue).

⚠️ MCQHigh-Yield
AREA 6 · PREMOTOR

Anterior to Area 4

Complex movements, postural adjustments. Contains: Broca's (speech), Frontal eye field, Head rotation area, Exner's (hand skills). Gives 30% to pyramidal + extrapyramidal fibers.

⚠️ MCQ🌟 Pearl
SUPPLEMENTAL MOTOR AREA

Above & Anterior to Area 6

Initiates complex bilateral movements. Provides postural background for fine skilled movements. Works with basal ganglia.

⚡ UMN vs LMN Lesion — Quick Compare

🔼 Upper Motor Neuron Lesion (UMNL)

  • Widespread hemiplegia (below lesion)
  • Hypertonia — spastic paralysis
  • Exaggerated (hyperactive) tendon jerks
  • Positive Babinski sign
  • NO early muscle wasting
  • Cause: stroke, capsular lesion

🔽 Lower Motor Neuron Lesion (LMNL)

  • Localized flaccid paralysis at lesion level
  • Hypotonia — flaccid paralysis
  • Absent (areflexia) tendon jerks
  • Negative Babinski sign
  • Marked EARLY muscle wasting + fasciculations
  • Cause: poliomyelitis, AHC damage
🏷️ Key Clinical Signs
Babinski Sign
Dorsiflexion of big toe on plantar stimulation. Positive = UMNL (pyramidal lesion). Normal in infants (unmyelinated tract).
⚠️ EXAM FAVORITE
Fanning (Babinski+)
Dorsiflexion + fanning of outer 4 toes = extrapyramidal tract also involved.
High-Yield
Spasticity
Hypertonia from loss of UMN inhibition on LMNs. Velocity-dependent resistance.
🌟 Pearl
Fasciculations
Spontaneous muscle fiber contractions visible under skin. Classic LMNL sign (denervation).
High-Yield
Motor Aphasia
Cannot articulate speech despite intact understanding. Broca's area (Area 6) lesion.
🌟 Pearl
Motor Apraxia
Inability to perform learned skilled movements. Exner's area (Area 6) lesion.
⚠️ MCQ
Lower Facial Palsy
Contralateral UMNL → only lower face paralyzed (7th nucleus lower half = contralateral only).
⚠️ EXAM
Reaction of Degeneration
LMNL: no Faradic response; Galvanic: ACC > CCC (anodal stronger than cathodal).
LMNL Specific
📊 Extrapyramidal Tracts Quick Reference
🔴 Rubrospinal

Origin: Red nucleus (midbrain). Function: Distal limb flexor tone. System: Lateral.

🟡 Reticulospinal

Medial (pontine): facilitates antigravity muscles. Lateral (medullary): inhibits antigravity muscles. System: Medial.

🔵 Vestibulospinal

Lateral: ipsilateral, facilitates antigravity/balance. Medial: bilateral, neck/upper limb position during acceleration. System: Medial.

🟢 Tectospinal

Origin: Superior + inferior colliculi (midbrain). Function: Reflex head turning to sudden visual/auditory stimuli. System: Medial. ⚠️ MCQ

🔄 Mechanism: UMNL Cascade
1
Stroke / Capsular lesion — blood supply to internal capsule interrupted, severing descending UMN axons.
2
Loss of cortical motor command — LMNs in spinal cord no longer receive activation signals → voluntary paralysis (hemiplegia) on contralateral side.
3
Loss of inhibitory control — UMNs normally inhibit primitive spinal reflexes. Disinhibition leads to hyperreflexia and spasticity (hypertonia).
4
Positive Babinski sign — pyramidal tract can no longer suppress primitive withdrawal reflex → dorsiflexion of big toe on plantar stimulation.
5
If extrapyramidal also involved — Babinski + fanning of outer 4 toes. Tone changes (may increase or decrease) depending on which extrapyramidal tracts are damaged.
🔄 Extrapyramidal General Pathway

Premotor Area 6 (mainly) + Area 4 → Corpus Striatum (Basal Ganglia) → Globus Pallidus → Brainstem Nuclei (Red / Reticular / Vestibular / Tectum) → Spinal Tracts → AHC → Muscle

💊 Clinical Management

🚨 Acute UMNL (Stroke) — Emergency Management

⚡ Immediate

  • Stabilize ABC (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
  • Neurological assessment — GCS, NIHSS
  • Emergency CT/MRI brain
  • Identify ischaemic vs haemorrhagic
  • Thrombolysis (tPA) if ischaemic <4.5h

🔬 Investigations

  • MRI brain — internal capsule lesion
  • Carotid Doppler — stenosis
  • ECG — AF source of emboli
  • FBC, coagulation screen
  • Echo — cardiac source

🛡️ Secondary Prevention

  • Antiplatelets (aspirin, clopidogrel)
  • Anticoagulation if AF
  • Statin therapy
  • BP control (<130/80 mmHg)
  • Lifestyle modification

🏋️ Rehabilitation

  • Physiotherapy — motor retraining
  • Occupational therapy — ADLs
  • Speech therapy if Broca's aphasia
  • Spasticity: baclofen, botulinum toxin
  • Neuroplasticity exercises

⬇️ LMNL Management (e.g., Poliomyelitis, AHC Disease)

🔬 Diagnosis Confirmation

  • EMG — denervation potentials
  • Nerve conduction studies
  • Reaction of degeneration test
  • Muscle biopsy if needed

🏋️ Muscle Preservation

  • Physiotherapy — prevent contractures
  • Splinting — maintain joint position
  • Galvanic stimulation — denervated muscle
  • Hydrotherapy

🔄 Nerve Regeneration Support

  • Neurotrophic factors (research)
  • Nutritional support — vitamins B1, B12
  • Nerve repair/grafting if applicable
  • Assistive devices — orthotics

💬 Broca's Aphasia Management (Area 6 Lesion)

🚨 Acute

  • Treat underlying stroke/lesion
  • Speech-language pathology ASAP
  • Communication aids (AAC devices)

🔄 Rehabilitation

  • Constraint-induced language therapy (CILT)
  • Melodic intonation therapy
  • Regular SLP sessions 5×/week acute phase
  • Family communication training
