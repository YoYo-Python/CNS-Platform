Complete diagnostic framework for language disorders

Definition of Aphasia ⭐ MCQ

Speech abnormality resulting from damage to specific language areas in the dominant hemisphere (usually from vascular stroke). It is NOT due to vision/hearing defects or muscle paralysis. Most common cause: vascular stroke.

Diagnostic Algorithm — Hearing vs. Reading Loss
1

Patient cannot understand spoken words

Lesion in Auditory Association Area (Area 22) → Auditory Agnosia, or in Wernicke's area → Wernicke's Aphasia

2

Patient cannot understand written words (reading only)

If vision is intact → Lesion in Angular Gyrus → Dyslexia/Word Blindness

3

Patient cannot understand written OR spoken words

Lesion in Wernicke's area itself → Wernicke's Aphasia (both inputs affected)

4

Patient understands but cannot speak fluently

Lesion in Broca's Area → Non-Fluent (Expressive) Aphasia

5

Patient cannot write despite normal hand motor function

Lesion in Exner's Area → Agraphia

6

Patient cannot recognize faces

Lesion in Medioventral Occipitotemporal Surface → Prosopagnosia
