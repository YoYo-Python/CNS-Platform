👅 Gustation

A chemical sense mediated by taste buds on the tongue and mouth mucosa. Taste buds reside in fungiform papillae (tip & edges) and circumvallate papillae (posterior V). Each bud contains 50–150 modified epithelial cells with a lifespan of ~10 days.

⚠️ EXAM: Taste buds decrease after age 45. Filliform papillae do NOT contain taste buds.
  • 🧂
    Salty
    NaCl → Epithelial Na⁺ channels open → Na⁺ influx → depolarization
  • 🍋
    Sour
    H⁺ binds & blocks K⁺ channels → depolarization
  • 🍬
    Sweet
    Organics → Gustatin (G-protein) → ↑cAMP → phosphorylates/closes K⁺ channels
  • ☕
    Bitter
    Alkaloids → ↓K⁺ conductance OR ↑IP₃ → releases internal Ca²⁺
  • 🥩
    Umami
    L-glutamate → mGluR4 → Na⁺ influx + K⁺ efflux

👃 Olfaction

A chemical sense for volatile, water-soluble, and lipid-soluble substances. Receptors are bipolar neurons in the olfactory mucosa (superior nasal concha) with a lifespan of ~60 days. Axons pierce the cribriform plate to synapse in the olfactory bulb.

⚠️ EXAM: Olfactory receptors are bipolar neurons (unlike taste cells which are modified epithelial cells). G-protein is Golf, NOT gustatin.

Clinical Significance
👶➡️👴
Age-related changes: Taste buds decrease after age 45. Over 75% of humans above age 80 have impaired smell identification (Hyposmia). This can lead to anorexia and malnutrition.
🧬
Addison's Disease: Adrenal insufficiency → Na⁺ loss → CNS-driven salt craving (taste preference). Adrenalectomized animals strongly prefer salty water.
🦷
Parathyroidectomy: Hypocalcemia → CNS-driven preference for calcium-containing water. Demonstrates taste preference is driven by bodily needs.
🦠
COVID-19: Typically causes loss of taste (ageusia) and loss of smell (anosmia), unlike Addison's which causes hypersensitivity + salt craving.
