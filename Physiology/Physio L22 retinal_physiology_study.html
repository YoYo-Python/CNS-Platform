🧬 Key Structure Cards

🖤

Pigmented Epithelium

Outermost physiological retinal layer. Contains melanin to absorb unabsorbed light, preventing reflection. Stores Vitamin A for photopigment recycling. Phagocytoses old outer segment tips.

⚠️ ExamVit A Storage
🌑

Rods

120 million. Use Rhodopsin (visual purple). Peripheral distribution. 300:1 convergence → high sensitivity, low accuracy. Mediate scotopic (night) vision. No color detection.

Scotopic300:1 ratio
☀️

Cones

6–7 million. Use Iodopsin (3 types = 3 colors). Central/foveal distribution. 1:1 convergence → high accuracy. Mediate photopic (day) vision. Color discrimination.

Photopic1:1 ratio
🎯

Fovea Centralis

1.5 mm depression in the 4.5 mm macula lutea. Contains ONLY cones. Layers shifted aside → direct light hit. 1:1 convergence. Avascular. Area of most acute vision.

⚠️ ExamOnly Cones
🕳️

Optic Disc (Blind Spot)

Located 4 mm medial to fovea. No photoreceptors — hence blind spot. Exit point for optic nerve axons and entry/exit for blood vessels. Clinically: papilledema (protrusion) or optic atrophy (depression).

⚠️ ExamPapilledema
🔗

Bipolar Cells (1st Order)

Transmit signals from photoreceptors → ganglion cells. 1st order neurons of the visual pathway. Inhibited by glutamate in the dark via Cl⁻ channels.

⚠️ MCQ1st Neuron
⚡

Ganglion Cells (2nd Order)

Only cells generating action potentials in the retinal pathway. Their axons form the optic nerve. 2nd order neurons transmitting signals to the brain.

Action Potential⚠️ MCQ
↔️

Horizontal Cells

Connect photoreceptors ↔ bipolar cells. Provide lateral inhibition — stops lateral spread of excitation. Increases visual contrast and accuracy.

Lateral Inhibition
🔀

Amacrine Cells

~30 types. Connect bipolar ↔ ganglion cells. Help analyze visual signals before they leave the retina (e.g., motion, onset/offset of light).

Signal Analysis
🌊

Dark Current

In darkness: high cGMP keeps Na⁺ channels open. Na⁺ flows in → -40 mV depolarization. Continuous glutamate release inhibits bipolar cells → no optic nerve firing.

-40 mV⚠️ Exam
💡

Light Response

Light → Metarhodopsin II → Transducin → PDE → ↓cGMP → closes Na⁺ channels → -80 mV hyperpolarization → ↓ glutamate → excites pathway.

⚠️ Exam-80 mV
🌓

Duplicity Theory

Retinal function is double in nature depending on its 2 photoreceptors. Photopic vision (cones, day, high accuracy, color) vs. Scotopic vision (rods, night, high sensitivity, no color).

🔄 Signal Transmission Timeline

Layer 1

Pigmented Epithelium

Absorbs stray light. Stores Vitamin A. Recycles all-trans retinal → 11-cis retinal via isomerase.

Layer 2

Photoreceptors (Rods & Cones)

Transduce light into electrical signals via phototransduction cascade in outer segment disks.

Layer 3

Bipolar Cells (1st Order Neurons)

Receive signals from photoreceptors. Modulated by horizontal cells. Pass signals to ganglion cells.

Layer 4

Ganglion Cells (2nd Order Neurons)

Generate action potentials. Axons form the optic nerve. Exit via optic disc to visual cortex.

🌙 Retinal Adaptation

Dark and light adaptation — mechanisms, timelines, and physiological changes

🌑 Dark Adaptation
  • Ability of retina to increase sensitivity to faint light (up to 100,000×)
  • Mechanism: Slow regeneration of photopigments (rhodopsin & iodopsin)
  • Cone adaptation: completes in 5 minutes
  • Rod adaptation: completes in 30 minutes
  • Pupil dilates to allow more light entry
  • Visual acuity decreases (no fine detail)
  • Peak luminous spectrum shifts to blue-green
  • All-trans retinal → pigmented epithelium → isomerase → 11-cis retinal → rhodopsin restored
☀️ Light Adaptation
  • Decrease in retinal sensitivity occurring in bright light
  • Mechanism: Rapid degeneration (breakdown) of photopigments
  • Completes within 5 minutes
  • Pupil constricts (miosis) to reduce light entry
  • Visual sensitivity decreases
  • Peak luminous spectrum shifts to yellow-green
  • Both rods and cones are affected

🔬 Clinical: Night Blindness (Nyctalopia)

🥕

Vitamin A Deficiency

Most common cause. Pigmented epithelium cannot store adequate Vitamin A → isomerase cannot regenerate 11-cis retinal → rhodopsin cannot reform → impaired dark adaptation → night blindness.

⚠️ High-Yield
📏

Assessment: Dark Adaptometry

Measures the threshold of light detection over time in the dark. Electroretinography (ERG) measures retinal electrical responses. Standard diagnostic tools for adaptation disorders.

Clinical Pearl
🌍

Scotopic vs Photopic Range

The retina can adapt to a difference of 10 billion times between starlight and sunlight. Dark adaptation contributes up to 100,000× sensitivity increase; pupil dilation adds ×16 more light.
