🗺️ Sensations at a Glance
🎯
What is Sensation?
The conscious perception of a particular feeling caused by stimulation of a receptor by its adequate stimulus. General vs. Special distinction is key.
High-YieldExam
✋
Crude Touch
Poorly localized — strong stimulus, ill-defined object (cotton). Carried by A-δ (5–30 m/s) & C fibers (0.5–2 m/s) via Ventral Spinothalamic Tract.
High-YieldPathway
🖐️
Fine Touch
Highly localized — precise object (pinhead). Meissner's (rapid) + Merkel's (slow). A-β fibers (30–70 m/s) via Dorsal Column–Medial Lemniscal System.
High-YieldExam-FavClinical Pearl
⚖️
Pressure & Vibration
Pacinian corpuscles → pressure + high-freq vibration (up to 700 Hz). Meissner's → low-freq vibration (<200 Hz). Both via Gracile & Cuneate tracts.
ExamPearl
🦵
Proprioception: Conscious
Body → Dorsal column (Gracile/Cuneate) → Cerebral Cortex. Face → Trigeminal pathway → Primary Sensory Area.
High-YieldExam
🧠
Proprioception: Subconscious
Body → Spinocerebellar tracts (Dorsal & Ventral) → Cerebellar Cortex. Regulates equilibrium & voluntary movement.
ExamPathway
🎵
Modality Coding
Peripheral: Müller's Law — each receptor type → one sensation. Central: Labelled Line Principle — specific receptor → specific tract → specific cortex.
High-YieldExam-Fav
📊
Intensity Coding
Peripheral: Rate of impulse discharge + Receptor recruitment. Central: Lateral inhibition (excites strong pathway, inhibits adjacent weak ones).
Clinical PearlMechanism
📍
Locality Coding
Peripheral: Receptive field size + Overlap. Central: Somatotopic map, Law of Projection, Lateral inhibition. Fingertip: 3mm, Back: 70mm minimal distance.
High-YieldExam
⚠️Exam Tip (Arabic): "كل نوع من السنسيشن هقول تبقى عارف الريسبتور بتاعتها ايه" — Know the exact receptors for EVERY sensation type. The 4 fine touch types are ALL carried by the Dorsal Medial Lemniscal system.

🔬 Clinical Testing & Diagnosis

Step-by-step neurological examination algorithm for sensory assessment

1
🏥 History & Complaint
Patient reports numbness, loss of sensation, inability to grip objects, balance problems. Document onset, location, progression, and associated symptoms.
2
✋ Crude Touch Testing
Apply cotton wool to skin bilaterally. Ask patient to report when touched. Inability indicates ventral spinothalamic tract lesion. Preserved even in dorsal column lesions.
Cotton Wool Test Bilateral Comparison
3
🖐️ Fine Touch: Two-Point Discrimination
Use calibrated compass. Normal: 3mm fingertips, 70mm back. Increased minimal distance → dorsal column dysfunction. Tests both Meissner's and Merkel's receptors.
Discrimination Compass Fingertips: 3mm Back: 70mm Tongue tip: 1mm
4
🔑 Stereognosis Test
Patient closes eyes. Place familiar objects (key, coin, pen) in their hand. Normal = identifies correctly. Astereognosis = inability to recognize → dorsal column/parietal cortex lesion.
Objects: key / coin / pen Eyes closed required
5
🎵 Vibration Sense Testing
128Hz tuning fork for low-frequency (Meissner's) / 512Hz for high-frequency (Pacinian). Place on bony prominences (medial malleolus, wrist). Loss → early dorsal column or peripheral nerve damage.
128 Hz fork → Meissner's 512 Hz fork → Pacinian Bony prominences
6
🦵 Proprioception (Joint Position Sense)
Move patient's toe/finger with eyes closed. Ask "up or down?" Normal = conscious awareness via dorsal columns. Loss + preserved crude touch = pure dorsal column lesion.
Eyes closed Up / Down judgment Romberg's sign if bilateral
7
🧠 Cortical Interpretation
If peripheral sensation is intact but patient still can't identify objects → lesion at cortical level. Somatotopic mapping ensures sensations are "projected" back to the body site via the Law of Projection.
Tactile Agnosia possible Law of Projection
