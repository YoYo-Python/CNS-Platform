📌 Key Concept Cards
🎯

Visceral Pain

Poorly localized, colicky, burning or dull aching pain from diffuse stimulation of visceral pain receptors. Sharp cuts do NOT cause visceral pain — diffuse stimuli do.

⚠️ ExamHigh-Yield
🔀

Referred Pain

Pain from a viscus felt in a somatic structure sharing the same embryonic dermatome. Explained by the Convergence-Projection Theory at the SGR dorsal horn cells.

⚠️ ExamClinical Pearl
🤢

Autonomic Signs of Visceral Pain

Severe visceral pain = parasympathetic manifestations: ↓HR, ↓BP, pupillary constriction, nausea & vomiting. NOT tachycardia.

⚠️ Exam
🛤️

Parietal vs Visceral Pathway

Parietal: A-δ & C fibers via somatic nerves → sharp, well-localized. Visceral: C fibers only via autonomic nerves → dull, poorly localized.

High-Yield
🧠

Brain Has No Pain Receptors!

Brain tissue is devoid of pain receptors. Only surrounding structures (meninges, blood vessels, venous sinuses, cranial nerves V, IX, X) are pain-sensitive.

⚠️ ExamClinical Pearl
⚡

Neuropathic Pain

Chronic pain from pathological nerve changes (peripheral or central). Excruciating paroxysms, burning/shooting quality, hyperalgesia. Barely responsive to standard treatment.

High-YieldClinical Pearl
🔒

Pain Analgesia System (PAS)

Descending inhibitory system: PAG → Enkephalins → GABA inhibition → NRM (serotonin) + Paragiagantocellularis (noradrenaline) → Dorsal horn enkephalins → Block Substance P & Glutamate.

⚠️ Exam
🚪

Gate Theory

SGR cells (layers II & III) act as a gate. A-β fibers (rubbing) and A-δ fibers (acupuncture) close the gate via presynaptic inhibition, reducing pain transmission.

High-Yield
💊

Endogenous Opioids

Enkephalins → Delta (δ) receptors · Endorphins (from POMC) → Mu (µ) receptors · Dynorphins → Sigma (σ) receptors. Morphine mimics these at the same receptors.

📊 Headache Types Timeline
👁️

Extracranial Headaches

• Refractive errors / glaucoma (eyes)
• Sinusitis (nose)
• Dental caries (oral)
• Psychogenic: emotional tension → scalp/neck muscle contraction

🧠

Intracranial Headaches

• Meninges (inflammation / traction)
• Blood vessels (over-distension)
• Venous sinuses
• Cranial nerves V, IX, X
• Tumors → pressure on pain-sensitive structures

Pearl: Brain itself has NO pain receptors
🌡️

Fever Headache Mechanism

Temperature + microbial toxins → over-distension (vasodilation) of cerebral arteries → stretch pain receptors in vessel walls → headache

💉

Post-Spinal Tap Headache

Removed CSF → reduced buoyancy → on standing, brain descends by gravity → traction on pain-sensitive meninges (dura, tentorium) → positional headache

Classic Vignette
📈

Hypertensive Headache

High BP → over-distension of cerebral arteries during each systole → throbbing pulsatile headache synchronized with the pulse

⚡

Migraine Headache

Vascular mechanism → cerebral artery changes. Preceded by aura (prodrome). Associated with photo/phonophobia. Throbbing, unilateral typically.

🚨 Emergency / Acute Pain Management

🚨 Exogenous Opioids (Urgent)

  • Morphine: targets Mu (µ) receptors — mimics endorphins
  • Provides presynaptic inhibition at dorsal horn
  • Blocks Substance P & Glutamate release
  • Only partial effect on neuropathic pain
  • Targets same receptors as endogenous opioids

🔴 Neuropathic Pain — Special Considerations

  • Standard NSAIDs largely ineffective
  • Opioids only partially effective
  • Anticonvulsants (e.g., gabapentin, carbamazepine for trigeminal neuralgia)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
  • Topical agents (lidocaine, capsaicin)
🟡 Headache Management by Type

🌡️ Fever Headache

  • Antipyretics (paracetamol, NSAIDs)
  • Treat underlying infection (antibiotics if bacterial)
  • Reversal of cerebral artery over-distension

📈 Hypertensive Headache

  • Antihypertensives to reduce BP
  • Reduces over-distension of cerebral arteries
  • Monitor for hypertensive emergency

💉 Post-Spinal Tap Headache

  • Lie flat (reduces gravity-induced traction)
  • Adequate hydration to replenish CSF
  • Blood patch (epidural) if severe/persistent
  • Caffeine (vasoconstrictive effect)

😤 Psychogenic / Tension Headache

  • Address underlying emotional stress/tension
  • Muscle relaxants for scalp/neck tension
  • Simple analgesics (NSAIDs, paracetamol)
  • Psychological counseling / biofeedback
🟢 Non-Pharmacological Pain Control

✋ Skin Rubbing (Gate Theory)

  • Stimulates A-β (tactile) fibers
  • Collaterals inhibit pain at SGR gate
  • Immediate but temporary relief
  • Spinal level mechanism
  • Classic: rubbing a bumped knee

📍 Acupuncture

  • Stimulates A-δ fibers with needles
  • Closes pain gate at SGR
  • Also activates descending PAS
  • May trigger endogenous opioid release

🧘 Cognitive / Stress Reduction

  • Reduces cortical input driving pain perception
  • Positive emotions modulate PAS activation
  • Reduces anxiety-related pain amplification

💉 Endogenous Opioid Activation

  • Exercise → β-endorphin release (Mu receptors)
  • Severe stress → enkephalin cascade via PAG
  • "Battlefield analgesia" — soldiers feel no pain acutely
  • Can be blocked by naloxone (opioid antagonist)
