Sleep & EEG — The Big Picture

Sleep is an active inhibitory process where the brain suppresses its own arousal circuits. Two fundamentally different sleep states alternate in 90-100 min cycles: Slow-Wave Sleep (SWS) — driven by serotonin and GABA — and REM (Paradoxical) Sleep — driven by acetylcholine. The EEG reflects brain state with four distinct wave patterns from fast (alert) to slow (deep sleep).

⚙️ SWS Initiation Flow
🟢 How Slow-Wave Sleep Begins
😴

Raphe Nucleus (Lower pons/medulla)

Serotonergic neurons release Serotonin (5-HT), which inhibits the Reticular Activating System (RAS) — shutting down the brain's arousal circuit.

🧠

Preoptic Nucleus (Anterior Hypothalamus)

Releases GABA onto histaminergic neurons in the posterior hypothalamus, silencing their excitatory drive to the thalamus and cortex.

🚫

Histamine Blocked

Posterior hypothalamus histaminergic neurons are suppressed → no histamine release → thalamus and cortex lose their wake-promoting excitation.

💤

SWS Induced

Cortical and thalamic activity slow down progressively through Stages 1→4 with theta and delta waves.

🔴 REM Sleep Control
🌀 REM ON / REM OFF Balance
🟣 REM ON (Initiates REM)
  • Cholinergic neurons in upper brainstem RF
  • Activated when serotonin/NA levels fall
  • Send excitatory signals → RAS, Thalamus, Cortex
  • Result: Beta-rhythm EEG + muscle atonia
🟢 REM OFF (Terminates REM)
  • Raphe nuclei — serotoninergic
  • Locus ceruleus — noradrenergic
  • Inhibit cholinergic REM ON neurons
  • Result: REM episode ends, cycle restarts
📌 Sleep-Promoting Brain Structures
🔵
Suprachiasmatic Nucleus
Promotes sleep and synchronises the sleep/wake rhythm with light-dark cycles (circadian clock).
🟢
Nucleus Tractus Solitarius
Stimulation leads to sleep induction; one of the subcortical sleep-promoting structures.
🟡
Preoptic Area (Ant. Hypo)
GABAergic inhibition of wake-promoting histaminergic neurons in posterior hypothalamus.
🔴
Raphe Nuclei
Dual role: serotonin initiates SWS; also part of REM OFF system to terminate REM.
