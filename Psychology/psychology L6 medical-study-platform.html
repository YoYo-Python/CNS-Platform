🔄 Psychological System — Sequence

1. Consciousness (الوعي)

Awareness of time, place, and people — the first assessment step

2. Attention (الانتباه)

Focusing attention on a topic or stimulus

3. Emotion (المشاعر)

Feelings and affective state — influenced by the encounter

4. Thinking (التفكير)

Organizing and processing thoughts logically

5. Perception (الإدراك)

Understanding and interpreting raw sensations (e.g., identifying a voice)

6. Memory / Cognitive Functions

Recall of past events and cognitive integration

7. Insight (البصيرة)

Recognizing one's own illness — absent in psychosis ⚠️

8. Judgment (الحكم)

Reacting appropriately to situations — the final output

✅ Professional Conduct — Step-by-Step

🚨 Aggressive Patient — Protocol

  • Remain calm — do NOT shout back
  • Maintain composed non-verbal demeanor
  • Practice assertiveness (right is right, politely)
  • Filter complaints for relevant medical info
  • Call hospital security if unsafe
  • Never engage in physical/verbal fight

📋 History Taking — Best Practice

  • Assess consciousness first (time/place/person)
  • Actively filter irrelevant side-chatter
  • Use clear, professional language — no slang
  • Extract precise medical information
  • Ensure all vital info is gathered
  • Document accurately to prevent malpractice

🧠 Psychiatric Patient — Approach

  • Assess insight first — do they recognize illness?
  • Obtain collateral history from relatives
  • Use non-verbal cues — calm, non-threatening
  • Involve family in communication plan
  • Extra patience and assertiveness required
  • Document carefully — legal implications

📚 Medical Student Excellence

  • Understand concepts — do not just memorize
  • Attend clinical rounds and see real patients
  • Practice history taking and physical examination
  • Develop empathy through patient interaction
  • Avoid exclusive focus on MCQ preparation
  • Translate knowledge into clinical judgment

💊 Verbal Communication — Protocol

  • Use precise, respectful, professional language
  • Avoid all street slang and colloquialisms
  • Explain prescriptions with 100% accuracy
  • Treat medical advice as strictly as religious texts
  • Ensure patient/relative understands the plan
  • Confirm understanding before ending consult

🤝 Building Doctor-Patient Trust

  • Maintain strict confidentiality at all times
  • Work in teams during high-risk procedures
  • Keep self-motivated despite hectic conditions
  • Balance verbal AND non-verbal communication
  • Show empathy — connect emotionally
  • Never share patient info on social media

📊 Clinical Encounter Algorithm

STEP 1
Patient Presents
Greet professionally. Observe non-verbal cues. Assess consciousness (oriented to time/place/person?).
↓
STEP 2
Filter & History Taking
Actively filter irrelevant talk. Use professional verbal language. Gather precise medical history.
↓
STEP 3
Physical Examination
Thorough exam. Use non-verbal reassurance. Maintain professional composure throughout.
↓
STEP 4
Accurate Diagnosis
Apply clinical understanding (not mere memorization). Avoid errors from missed history steps.
↓
STEP 5
Communicate Treatment Plan
With integrity and honesty. Use clear language. Involve relatives. Confirm patient understanding.
↓
OUTCOME
Patient Confidence & Compliance ✅
Good communication → easier diagnosis → patient trust → compliance → better outcomes. Failure → malpractice.
