📚 High-Yield Notes

🔔 Classical Conditioning
▼

Learning in which a neutral stimulus (CS) is paired with an unconditioned stimulus (UCS) to elicit a conditioned response (CR).

Before: Neutral Stimulus → No Response.
During: NS + UCS → Unconditioned Response (UCR).
After: Conditioned Stimulus (CS) → Conditioned Response (CR).

Timing of pairings, stimulus generalization, stimulus discrimination. Responses are involuntary/autonomic. Developed by Ivan Pavlov.

Explains acquired fears (Little Albert), immune system suppression, and paraphilias.

⚠️ EXAM

The slide differentiating between Classical and Operant Conditioning is very important; it was a question that always came in exams as written comparisons.

⚙️ Operant Conditioning
▼

Learning where the consequences of behavior lead to a change in the probability of its occurrence. Developed by B.F. Skinner.

Active Behavior (Operant) → Environmental Consequence → Reinforcement (increases behavior) OR Punishment (decreases behavior). Responses are voluntary/somatic.

Positive Reinforcement: Add favorable outcome → behavior ↑
Negative Reinforcement: Remove unfavorable outcome → behavior ↑
Positive Punishment: Add unfavorable outcome → behavior ↓
Negative Punishment: Remove favorable outcome → behavior ↓

Continuous: Fastest learning, fastest extinction.
Intermittent (Fixed/Variable Ratio/Interval): Slower learning, highly resistant to extinction.

⚠️ EXAM

The slide regarding types of responses following behavior (Positive/Negative Reinforcement and Punishment) is very, very important. Comes as small written questions AND MCQs.

🐱 Trial and Error Learning (Thorndike)
▼

Learning through random responses until a satisfying outcome is achieved. Developed by Thorndike using cats in a puzzle box.

Hungry cat in a puzzle box with food outside. Scratches randomly until it hits the latch. Time to escape decreases over repeated trials (Law of Effect).

Association between the satisfying response and the stimulus. Without reward (food), latencies do not decrease.

🔁 Extinction, Generalization & Discrimination
▼

Disappearance of a conditioned response when the CS is no longer paired with the UCS.

Responding similarly to stimuli that resemble the conditioned stimulus (e.g., Little Albert feared all white fluffy objects).

Learning to distinguish between various distinct stimuli; responding only to the CS and not to similar stimuli.

Rewarding successive, closer approximations of the desired target behavior (Operant Conditioning).

😔 Learned Helplessness
▼

An animal repeatedly subjected to inescapable aversive stimuli becomes apathetic and passive, even when escape becomes possible.

Serves as an animal model for depression. Treatment: antidepressants restore escape attempts.

💊 Behavioral Therapies (Clinical Applications)
▼

Pairs relaxation with increasing doses of a fear-provoking stimulus (hierarchy of fears). Used for phobias.

Immediate exposure to actual overwhelming fear stimulus. Habituation extinguishes fear.

Imagined (not actual) exposure to an overwhelming fear stimulus.

Pairs maladaptive pleasurable behavior with painful stimuli (e.g., pedophilia + electric shocks). Classical conditioning basis.

Positive reinforcement using tokens for desirable behaviors in disorganized/psychotic/autistic patients. Tokens exchangeable for rewards.

Using physiological information (e.g., blood pressure monitor) to teach voluntary control of visceral changes.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: replacing distorted negative thoughts with positive ones. Used in depression and anxiety.

😱 Fear vs. Phobia
▼

Normal, proportionate emotional response to a perceived threat.

Irrational, exaggerated fear that causes panic even upon imagining the object. Disrupts daily life. Treated via Systematic Desensitization, Flooding, or Implosion.

📖 learning PPT
⚠️ Dangers of Physical Punishment
▼

Aggression, emotional trauma, modeling of aggressive behavior, mistrust, physical harm. Can lead to learned helplessness if excessive.

May be inconsistent, may model the very behavior being punished, and does not teach alternative positive behavior.

🧬 Classical Conditioning of the Immune System
▼

Rats drink sweet water (NS) paired with a drug (UCS) that suppresses immune cells (UCR). Sweet water alone eventually suppresses immunity → CS → CR.

Demonstrates that classical conditioning extends beyond behavioral responses to physical/immune system changes.

⚠️ Core Distinction

  • Classical = involuntary/autonomic, stimuli association (Pavlov) EXAM
  • Operant = voluntary/somatic, consequences of behavior (Skinner) EXAM
  • Thorndike = Trial & Error — cats in puzzle box
  • Watson = Little Albert — classical fear conditioning

⚠️ Reinforcement vs. Punishment

  • Positive Reinforcement: ADD reward → behavior ↑ EXAM
  • Negative Reinforcement: REMOVE bad → behavior ↑ EXAM
  • Positive Punishment: ADD bad → behavior ↓
  • Negative Punishment: REMOVE good → behavior ↓
  • BOTH reinforcements INCREASE behavior
  • BOTH punishments DECREASE behavior

Schedules of Reinforcement

  • Continuous: fast learning, fast extinction
  • Intermittent: slow learning, RESISTANT to extinction
  • Variable Ratio: most resistant to extinction (gambling)
  • Fixed Ratio: after set # of responses

Classical Conditioning Terms

  • UCS: evokes response automatically
  • UCR: automatic/unlearned response
  • CS: previously neutral, now elicits response
  • CR: learned response to CS
  • Extinction: CS without UCS → CR disappears
  • Generalization: respond to stimuli resembling CS

Behavioral Therapies

  • Systematic Desensitization: relaxation + gradual hierarchy
  • Flooding: actual overwhelming stimulus — immediate
  • Implosion: imagined overwhelming stimulus
  • Aversive Conditioning: pair bad behavior + pain
  • Token Economy: tokens for psychotic/autistic pts
  • Biofeedback: physiological data → visceral control
  • CBT: replace negative thoughts with positive

High-Yield Concepts

  • Learned Helplessness = model for DEPRESSION EXAM
  • Shaping: reward successive approximations
  • Phobia: irrational, exaggerated, causes panic on imagination
  • Punishment risks: aggression, trauma, modeling
  • Immune conditioning: CS can suppress immunity

Punishment Dangers

  • Models aggressive behavior EXAM
  • Emotional trauma and mistrust
  • Physical harm possible
  • Inconsistency reduces effectiveness
  • Does NOT teach alternative behaviors

Clinical Links

  • Paraphilias ← Classical conditioning
  • Acquired fears ← Little Albert
  • Depression ← Learned helplessness
  • Phobia Tx ← Systematic desensitization
  • Disorganized pts ← Token economy
  • Hypertension Tx ← Biofeedback
