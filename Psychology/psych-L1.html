Foundations of Psychology

⚠️ Exam Topic High Yield 4 Goals 5 Perspectives

Psychology is the science of behavior and mental processes. From Aristotle's concept of psyche (mind/breath) to modern clinical practice, this field uses observable behavior to draw inferences about private mental processes — and applies them to predict, understand, and change human experience.

📖 Core Definitions

🧬 Psychology

The Science of behavior and mental processes. Term derives from Aristotle's psyche = essence of life (mind/breath).

Definition

👁️ Behavior

Overt actions that can be directly observed by others. Examples: walking, speaking, facial expressions.

Observable

💭 Mental Processes

Private thoughts, emotions, feelings, and motives — cannot be directly observed. Inferred from behavior.

Private/Inferred
🎯 Four Goals of Psychology
⚠️ Exam Tip: Questions often come as vignette/scenario — identify which goal is being fulfilled. Easy marks if you understand the concept!
1

📊 Describe

Gathering information to accurately describe behavior. Example: Surveying hashish consumption among students.

2

🔮 Predict

Using personality traits to forecast future behavior or onset of illness before it occurs.

3

💡 Understand

Explaining behavior and mental processes using theories or hypotheses.

4

✏️ Change

Influencing/modifying behavior beneficially. Example: Helping a teenager overcome depression.

🏛️ Subfields of Psychology
⚠️ Exam Tip: Subfield questions will be clear and direct in the exam, despite some overlap between fields.

💓 Health Psychology

Explores the relationship between psychological factors (e.g., stress) and physical disease (e.g., ulcers, hypertension).

🩺 Clinical Psychology

Diagnosis and treatment of psychological disorders — from everyday crises to severe depression.

👥 Social Psychology

How people's thoughts, feelings, and actions are affected by others. Includes aggression and prejudice.

🌍 Cross-Cultural Psychology

Similarities/differences in psychological functioning across cultures. Fish picture experiment: American = leadership vs Chinese = being chased.

🧪 Clinical Neuropsychology

Unites neuroscience + clinical psychology to focus on biological origins of psychological disorders.

🌐 Five Perspectives
🧬

Neuroscience

How genetics and brain/nervous system function influence behavior.

🛋️

Psychodynamic (Freud)

Behavior driven by inner forces, unconscious activity, and conflicts from childhood.

⚙️

Behavioral

Observable behavior shaped by reinforcement and consequences (rewards/punishments). Application: Learned Helplessness model for depression.

🤔

Cognitive

Behavior is a reflection of thoughts. Change the thought → change the behavior.

🌱

Humanistic

Individuals naturally strive to grow, reach fulfillment, and be in control of their lives.

💊 Psychological Interventions
🚨 High-Yield: Learned Helplessness Treatment
💊 Pharmacological

Antidepressants — demonstrated to increase escape attempts in learned helplessness animal models. This validates learned helplessness as an animal model for clinical depression. Key evidence that depression has a biological (CNS) component responsive to pharmacotherapy.

🧠 Psychological

Cognitive restructuring — identifying and correcting negative thought patterns (cognitive perspective). Behavioral retraining — gradually exposing the subject to situations where they CAN succeed, unlearning the helplessness association.

🎯 Goal-Based Interventions
Goal: Describe

📊 Needs Assessment

Surveys, questionnaires, observational studies. E.g., measuring hashish consumption frequency.

Goal: Predict

🔮 Psychological Testing

Personality assessments, risk screening tools to predict disease onset or behavioral outcomes.

Goal: Understand

💡 Theory Building

Research, hypothesis testing, developing explanatory frameworks for behavior.

Goal: Change

✏️ Behavior Modification

Therapy, CBT, pharmacotherapy, social programs. E.g., treating teenage depression.

Health Psychology

💓 Stress Management

Psychological interventions to reduce physical disease burden — managing stress to lower hypertension, IBD flares, etc.

Divergent Thinking

🌊 Creative Problem Solving

Breaking mental sets. Applying unconventional solutions — e.g., water hoses vs. explosives for the Bar Lev line.
