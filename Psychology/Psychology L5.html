📋 Quick Summary Cards

🌱 Early Adulthood (20-40 yrs)

Peak physical development, independence, family formation. Erikson: Intimacy vs. Isolation. Failure → emotional isolation and inability to form lasting bonds.

⚡ Middle Adulthood (40-65 yrs)

Peak power and authority. Erikson: Generativity vs. Stagnation. Failure → mid-life crisis (career change, infidelity, depression). Climacterium affects both sexes.

🌅 Old Age (>65 yrs)

Functional decline, reflection. Erikson: Ego Integrity vs. Despair. Depression is the #1 psychiatric disorder. Pseudo-dementia frequently misdiagnosed as Alzheimer's.

🕊️ End of Life & Grief

Kübler-Ross stages: Denial → Anger → Bargaining → Depression → Acceptance. Normal grief resolves in 1-2 years. Abnormal grief: >2 months, >5% weight loss, intense guilt.

⚠️ HIGH-YIELD EXAM POINT
Depression is the most common psychiatric disorder in the elderly and can mimic Alzheimer's disease — called "Pseudo-dementia." Always evaluate depression before diagnosing dementia!
⚠️ HIGH-YIELD EXAM POINT
Abnormal Grief is distinguished from normal grief by: significant weight loss (>5%), profound guilt & worthlessness, inability to return to work, and duration exceeding 2 months.
⏳ Lifespan Developmental Timeline

🌱 Early Adulthood (20–40 years)

Peak physical and cognitive development. Establishing career, forming intimate relationships. Psychosocial crisis: Intimacy vs. Isolation. Success = deep relationships; failure = loneliness.

⚡ Middle Adulthood (40–65 years)

Maximum authority and productivity. Climacterium (menopause in women, decreased strength in men). Risk of mid-life crisis: career upheaval, infidelity, substance use. Psychosocial crisis: Generativity vs. Stagnation.

🌅 Old Age (65+ years)

Structural brain changes (enlarged ventricles, amyloid plaques). Cognitive slowing but intelligence preserved. Depression is the #1 psych disorder. Psychosocial crisis: Ego Integrity vs. Despair.

🕊️ End of Life

Kübler-Ross stages of dying. Normal grief resolution in 1–2 years. Abnormal grief requires pharmacological intervention (SSRIs, ECT). Anniversary reactions may persist.

🏷️ Key Clinical Concepts

🧠 Pseudo-Dementia

Depression in elderly mimicking Alzheimer's. Treatable with antidepressants/ECT. Most commonly misdiagnosed condition in old age.

🩺 Diagnostic Criteria & Algorithms

✅ Normal Grief — Features

  • ✓ Initial shock and denial
  • ✓ Sadness and crying spells
  • ✓ Minor weight loss
  • ✓ Mild feelings of guilt ("I wish I had visited more")
  • ✓ Illusion of deceased's presence (non-pathological)
  • ✓ Return to work/social activities
  • ✓ Resolves within ~2 months (subsides 1-2 years)
  • ✓ Anniversary reactions may recur

⚠️ Abnormal Grief — Diagnostic Criteria

  • ✗ Significant weight loss >5% body weight
  • ✗ Intense feelings of guilt and worthlessness
  • ✗ Severe symptoms persist >2 months
  • ✗ Inability to return to work or social activities
  • ✗ Psychomotor retardation
  • ✗ Suicidal ideation — HIGH RISK
  • ✗ Classification: A variety of depressive illness

🧠 Normal Aging vs. Alzheimer's Dementia — Investigation Findings

🔄 Diagnostic Algorithm: Cognitive Decline in the Elderly
👴 Elderly patient presents with memory complaints
⬇️
📋 Screen for Depression First

Loss of interest, sadness, guilt, weight changes, suicidal ideation?

⬇️
Depression present?

→ Consider Pseudo-Dementia. Trial of SSRIs. If cognition improves → Confirmed.

OR
No depression?

→ Evaluate for Alzheimer's Dementia. Neuroimaging, cognitive testing, ACh levels.

💊 Treatment & Management

🚨 Abnormal Grief — Acute Management

  • 💊 SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors) — first-line antidepressant
  • 💊 Antipsychotics — if psychotic features present
  • ⚡ ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy) — if suicidal, drug-resistant, or rapid response needed
  • 🤝 Increased physician contact — supportive relationship
  • 🗣️ Psychotherapy — grief counseling

🚨 Depression in the Elderly (Pseudo-Dementia)

  • 💊 SSRIs — first-line; avoid TCAs (anticholinergic side effects in elderly)
  • ⚡ ECT — effective and safe in elderly; especially if suicidal
  • 🗣️ Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Social support — combat isolation
  • ⚠️ Monitor for suicide risk — highest in elderly males

🌡️ Climacterium Management

  • 💊 Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) — for severe vasomotor symptoms
  • 🧠 Counseling — adaptation to life changes
  • 🏃 Exercise — maintains muscle mass and mood
  • 😌 Stress management — relaxation techniques

🦴 Somatic Complications of Aging

  • 🥛 Calcium-rich diet — osteoporosis prevention
  • 🏃 Regular exercise — bone density and cardiovascular health
  • ⚠️ Avoid sedatives — fall risk → hip fractures
  • 💤 Sleep hygiene — address insomnia without benzodiazepines
  • 👁️ Regular screenings — vision, hearing, cardiac function

⚠️ Physician's Role in End-of-Life Care

  • 🗣️ Communicate diagnosis honestly — patient has the right to know
  • 😤 Do not take anger personally — it's Stage 2 of dying
  • 🤝 Maintain empathy — avoid emotional detachment
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Support the family — they also go through grief stages
  • 📋 Discuss palliative care options and advance directives
