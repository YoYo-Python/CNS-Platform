Parkinson's Disease
Pharmacological Management

Comprehensive overview of pathophysiology, key drugs, and exam high-yields

🧠 Core Pathophysiology
⚡
Nigrostriatal Pathway: Progressive degeneration of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra pars compacta → loss of striatal dopamine → unopposed ACh activity → TRAP symptoms
🔴
Lewy Bodies: Intraneuronal inclusions containing α-synuclein — the hallmark pathology of PD
⚖️
Key Imbalance: ↓ Dopamine + ↑ Acetylcholine = Extrapyramidal dysfunction. Treatment restores this balance.
🧬
Etiology: Genetic (α-synuclein, Parkin mutations) + Environmental (MPTP, pesticides)
🎯 Clinical Triad — TRAP
🤲
T — Tremor: Resting ("pill-rolling"), 4–6 Hz, suppressed by voluntary movement. Best treated by anticholinergics.
💪
R — Rigidity: "Lead pipe" or "cogwheel" rigidity. Due to increased muscle tone from dopamine deficiency.
🐌
A — Akinesia/Bradykinesia: Slowness of movement. Best treated by dopaminergic agents. NOT responsive to anticholinergics.
🚶
P — Postural Instability: Late feature. Festinating gait, falls risk. Multifactorial.
🔵 Nigrostriatal
SNc → Striatum. Motor control via D2R. Deficit → Parkinson's. Excess → Dyskinesia. Blockade → EPS/Drug-induced PD.
🟣 Mesolimbic/Cortical
VTA → Limbic/Cortex. Reward & emotion. Excess → Schizophrenia/Hallucinations. L-dopa excess causes psychosis.
🟠 Tuberoinfundibular
Hypothalamus → Pituitary. Regulates prolactin. Blockade → Hyperprolactinemia (galactorrhea). Deficit → PRL suppression.
⚠️ Exam High-Yields
• Dopamine can NOT cross BBB
• Carbidopa = peripheral AAAD inhibitor
• Tolcapone = hepatotoxic
• Selegiline = insomnia (amphetamine-like)
• Vit B6 worsens L-dopa

💊 Drug Classes at a Glance

🔵 Dopamine Precursors
Levodopa (+Carbidopa as Sinemet). Gold standard. Crosses BBB as prodrug. 75% dose reduction with Carbidopa.
EXAM ⭐
🟣 Enzyme Inhibitors
COMT inhibitors (Entacapone, Tolcapone) — prevent 3-OMD formation. MAO-B inhibitors (Selegiline, Rasagiline) — prevent central dopamine breakdown.
HIGH YIELD
🟢 DA Agonists
Direct D2 agonists. Ergot: Bromocriptine (vasospasm risk). Non-ergot: Pramipexole, Apomorphine (for acute freezing).
CLINICAL PEARL
🟠 Amantadine
Dual MOA: DA releaser + NMDA antagonist. Antiviral origin. Causes livedo reticularis (characteristic rash).
MECHANISM
🔴 Anticholinergics
Benztropine, Trihexyphenidyl. Central M2 blockers. Good for tremor & sialorrhea. Contraindicated in BPH, glaucoma, dementia.
EXAM ⭐
🔵 Istradefylline
Adenosine A2 receptor antagonist. Add-on to Sinemet to reduce motor fluctuations. Novel mechanism distinct from dopaminergic drugs.

🔄 Disease Mechanism — Step by Step
⚙️ Direct Pathway (facilitates movement)
Cortex → Striatum → (D1 stimulation → inhibit GPi) → Thalamus disinhibited → Cortical activation → Movement facilitated ✅
⚙️ Indirect Pathway (inhibits movement)
Cortex → Striatum → (D2 stimulation → inhibit GPe) → STN disinhibited → activates GPi → Thalamus inhibited → Movement reduced ❌

🔄 Treatment Selection Algorithm

1
New Diagnosis of PD (Young patient, mild symptoms): Start MAO-B inhibitor (Selegiline/Rasagiline) as monotherapy to delay L-dopa initiation. Rationale: Delay L-dopa to postpone dyskinesias.
↓
2
Moderate symptoms requiring more efficacy: Initiate Levodopa + Carbidopa (Sinemet). Add COMT inhibitor (Entacapone) → Stalevo® triple combination. Rationale: COMT inhibitor prolongs L-dopa half-life, smooths "wearing-off".
↓
3
Motor fluctuations / Wearing-off: Shorten dose intervals of Sinemet. Add dopamine agonist (Pramipexole). Consider Istradefylline (A2 antagonist). Wearing-off = predictable. On-off = unpredictable.
↓
4
Acute freezing episodes (acute hypomobility): Subcutaneous Apomorphine injection. Works in 5–10 minutes. ⭐ EXAM: Apomorphine = rapid rescue for freezing.
↓
5
Dominant tremor + sialorrhea: Add central anticholinergic (Benztropine/Trihexyphenidyl). ⚠️ Contraindicated in BPH, glaucoma, dementia!
↓
6
Drug-induced Parkinsonism (antipsychotics): Use anticholinergics only. Do NOT add dopaminergic drugs — will worsen psychosis.
