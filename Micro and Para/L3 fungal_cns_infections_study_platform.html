🗺 Overview Key concepts at a glance

🦠 Cryptococcus neoformans

  • Type: Capsulated true yeast
  • Source: Pigeon droppings (excreta)
  • Route: Inhalation → lung → CNS
  • Host: HIV/AIDS, transplant, steroids
  • Key virulence: Polysaccharide capsule
  • CNS disease: Subacute/chronic meningoencephalitis
  • Most common fungal CNS infection

🔴 Mucormycosis (Zygomycosis)

  • Organisms: Rhizopus, Rhizomucor, Mucor
  • Type: Opportunistic filamentous mycosis
  • Route: Inhalation → nasal/lung hyphae
  • Host: DKA, burns, immunosuppressed
  • Key virulence: Angioinvasion (angiotrophic)
  • CNS disease: Rhino-cerebral (sinuses→eye→brain)
  • Requires "free iron" to germinate
⚠️
EXAM PEARL: "Crypto" = hidden — the capsule hides the fungus from macrophages. CD4+ T-cells are essential to overcome this evasion, explaining high risk in AIDS patients.
⚠️
EXAM PEARL: Pigeons don't get Cryptococcus because their body temperature (40–42°C) is too high for the fungus to reproduce. Optimal growth temp = 37°C.
⚠️
EXAM PEARL (DKA + Mucor): DKA lowers blood pH → reduces iron binding to transport proteins → increases "free iron" → fuels germination of Mucor spores into invasive hyphae.

🦠 Fungal Taxonomy
True Yeast

Cryptococcus neoformans

Budding yeast, 37°C, polysaccharide capsule, urease (+)

Filamentous Fungi

Mucor / Rhizopus / Aspergillus

Broad non-septate hyphae (Mucor), sporangiospores, angiotrophic

Yeast-like

Candida species

Yeast-like morphology, pseudohyphae, opportunistic

Dimorphic

Histoplasma / Blastomyces

Mold at 25°C (environment) → Yeast at 37°C (host tissue)

🔁 Diagnostic Algorithm

1
Clinical Presentation: Immunocompromised patient with headache, fever, confusion → suspect fungal CNS infection
↓
2
Risk Factor Assessment: HIV/AIDS → prioritize Cryptococcus | DKA + facial necrosis → prioritize Mucormycosis
↓
3
CSF Analysis (LP): High protein + low glucose + 90% lymphocytes → fungal meningitis pattern
↓
4
India Ink Stain (CSF): Capsulated yeast with clear halo → Cryptococcus confirmed
↓
5
Latex Agglutination (CSF): Rapid detection of cryptococcal polysaccharide antigen — useful AND rapid
↓
6
Culture on SDA (without cycloheximide): Creamy mucoid = Crypto | Gray-black "dirty snow" = Mucor
↓
7
KOH Mount (tissue/discharge): Broad, non-septate branching hyphae + sporangia = Mucormycosis
↓
8
Biochemical Confirmation: Urease (+) = C. neoformans
💊 Treatment Urgency-coded management

🚨 Emergency / Acute Management

🚨 Cryptococcal Meningitis

Induction: Amphotericin B + Flucytosine (5-FC)
Consolidation: Fluconazole (8 weeks)
Maintenance: Fluconazole (suppress relapse in HIV)

🚨 Rhino-cerebral Mucormycosis

Antifungal: Amphotericin B (drug of choice)
Must also: Treat underlying DKA/acidosis
Surgery mandatory: Debridement of necrotic tissue

🔑 Primary Antifungal Drugs

DrugUseMechanismNotes

🛠 Supportive / Additional Management

🧠 ICP Management (Crypto)

Serial lumbar punctures to relieve raised intracranial pressure. Consider external ventricular drain if refractory.

🔪 Surgical Debridement (Mucor)

Aggressive surgical removal of all necrotic tissue is mandatory. Antifungal alone is insufficient.

💊 Treat Underlying Cause

Mucormycosis: Correct DKA (IV insulin + fluids). Cryptococcosis: Optimize ART in HIV patients.

🛡 Prophylaxis

In advanced HIV (CD4 <100): Fluconazole prophylaxis to prevent cryptococcal meningitis.
