📌 Quick Summary

Core concepts from the intelligence & creativity lecture — at a glance.

🧠
Intelligence Defined
The global capacity to act purposefully, think rationally, and deal effectively with one's environment. Measured as IQ = MA/CA × 100. Peaks at age 26.
⚠️ EXAM HIGH-YIELD
🎭
Multiple Intelligences
Gardner's 7 types: Logical-mathematical, Linguistic, Spatial, Bodily-kinesthetic, Musical, Interpersonal, Intrapersonal. Explains diverse capabilities.
DEFINITION
⚠️
MR vs Dementia
IQ < 70 before age 18 = Mental Retardation. Decline after age 18 = Dementia. Key age cut-off: 18 years.
⚠️ EXAM HIGH-YIELD
🧬
Genetic Causes of MR
Down syndrome (chr 21), Fragile-X (FMR-1, most common inherited), Prader-Willi/Angelman (chr 15), PKU (single-gene metabolism error).
HIGH-YIELD
💡
The Creative Process
5 stages: Preparation → Incubation → Intimation → Illumination → Verification. Produces something original and worthwhile.
CLINICAL PEARL
✨
Giftedness & Savantism
Giftedness = significantly above-average intelligence (innate). Savantism = exceptional single-field performance, often in autism. Formula-driven defense strategy.
CLINICAL PEARL

📊 IQ Classification Timeline

🌟 Genius — IQ > 140
Exceptionally rare; extraordinary intellectual capacity.
🏆 Very Superior — IQ 120-139
Well above average; strong analytical and creative abilities.
⭐ Superior — IQ 110-119
Above average intellectual functioning.
📊 Average — IQ 90-109
The majority of the population. Two-thirds fall in 85-115 range.
📉 Dull Normal — IQ 80-89
Below average but not clinically impaired.
🔻 Borderline — IQ 70-79
Borderline intellectual functioning; threshold zone.
🚨 Mental Retardation — IQ < 70
Mild (50-70) | Moderate (35-50) | Severe (20-35) | Profound (<20). Onset before age 18.

🔑 Key Clinical Signs & Concepts

HIGH-YIELD
MR Clinical Features
Learning problems, language delay, persistence of infantile behavior, lack of social inhibitors, abnormal behaviors, poor self-care.
PEARL
Giftedness Traits
Intellectual curiosity, rapid learning, extreme intensity, advanced vocabulary, nonconformity, advanced empathy.
EXAM
Wechsler Tests
WAIS-IV (16-90yr) | WISC (6-16yr) | WPPSI (2½-7yr 3mo). Full-Scale IQ from VIQ + PIQ components.
DEFINITION
Raven's Matrices
60 visual analogy problems. Strictly non-verbal. Progressively harder. Measures general intelligence (g-factor).
EXAM
Normal Distribution
Two-thirds (66.6%) of population fall within IQ 85-115. 95% fall within 70-130.
HIGH-YIELD
Intelligence Peak
Both fluid and crystallized intelligence peak at age 26, followed by slow decline.

🔍 Diagnostic Framework

Tests, criteria, and investigation findings for intelligence assessment.

📋
Diagnostic Criteria for Mental Retardation
ICD / DSM Framework
  • Criterion 1: IQ score under 70 (2+ standard deviations below the mean)
  • Criterion 2: Onset before the age of 18 (adulthood)
  • Criterion 3: Significant deficits in adaptive functioning (learning, language, social rules, self-care)
⚠️ KEY DISTINCTION: If onset is AFTER 18 → Diagnose as Dementia, not MR. This WILL appear as an MCQ.

🧬 Genetic Investigation Algorithm

Step 1
🏥 Clinical Presentation
Child presents with delayed language, learning problems, persistence of infantile behavior, lack of social inhibitors
⬇️
Step 2
📊 Intelligence Testing
Administer WISC (6-16yr) or WPPSI (2½-7yr). Confirm IQ < 70.
⬇️
Step 3
🧬 Genetic Testing
Karyotype → Check for trisomy 21 (Down), chr 15 abnormalities (Prader-Willi/Angelman). FMR-1 gene testing for Fragile-X. Metabolic screening for PKU.
⬇️
Step 4
🔎 Acquired Causes Workup
Rule out infection, birth trauma, or other prenatal/perinatal insults if genetic tests negative.
⬇️
Step 5
📝 Classify & Plan
Classify severity (Mild/Moderate/Severe/Profound). Initiate behavioral therapy to maximize skill acquisition within IQ limits.

💊 Management & Intervention

Key approaches to managing intellectual disability and optimizing potential.

⚠️ CRITICAL CONCEPT: IQ cannot be increased via therapy. Behavioral therapy maximizes skill acquisition within the patient's fixed IQ capacity limit.
🚨 Essential Principles
IQ is Fixed
The underlying IQ level in MR cannot be raised or cured through any intervention. Therapy works within limits.
Early Detection
Onset must be identified before age 18 for MR diagnosis. Newborn screening (e.g., PKU) enables early metabolic intervention.
🧠 Behavioral Therapy
Skill Maximization
Help the patient achieve the absolute maximum skills and adaptive behaviors possible within their specific IQ range.
Social Skills Training
Address lack of social inhibitors, teach appropriate social behaviors, and improve daily functioning.
Language Therapy
Speech and language intervention for delayed language development — a core feature of MR.
🤝 Supportive Measures
Special Education
Tailored educational programs matched to the patient's severity level (Mild → educable; Moderate → trainable).
Family Counseling
Support for family members in understanding the diagnosis, expectations, and caregiving strategies.
Self-Care Training
Systematic training for activities of daily living (ADLs) — especially critical in moderate-to-profound MR.
🧬 Preventive / Metabolic
PKU Dietary Management
Phenylalanine-restricted diet from birth prevents MR in PKU. Example of an inborn error of metabolism where early intervention changes outcome.
Genetic Counseling
For families with heritable causes (esp. Fragile-X as most common inherited cause) — assess recurrence risk.
