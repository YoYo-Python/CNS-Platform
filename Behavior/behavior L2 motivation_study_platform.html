⚠️ High-Yield Exam Facts

Definition: Motivation = internal state that activates and gives direction to thoughts, feelings, and actions.
Incentive: External cue that activates hunger motivation (e.g., smell of food).
Locus of Control: Internal → greater success. External → blame circumstances/others.
Hypothalamus: Master regulator. Lateral = hunger on. Ventromedial = hunger off. Paraventricular = blood sugar control.
Opponent Process: Repeated use → blunted positive, intensified withdrawal → motivation shifts from pleasure-seeking to pain-avoidance.

Key Structures

Hunger Regulation Flowchart

⬇️ Blood Glucose Drops / Stomach Contractions
↓
Liver & Duodenum sensors detect low glucose → signal brain
↓
🔴 Lateral Hypothalamus activated → "START EATING" signal
↓
Food consumed → blood glucose rises
↓
🟢 Ventromedial Hypothalamus activated → "STOP EATING" signal
↓
✅ Satiety achieved — Homeostasis restored
↓
📊 Long-term: Fat cells secrete Leptin → Paraventricular Nucleus decreases appetite

Solomon's Opponent-Process (Addiction)

💉 First Heroin Use → Intense Euphoria (Process A)
↓
Opponent system activates → mild dysphoria (Process B) after drug wears off
↓
⚡ Repeated Use → Process A weakens (tolerance) — less euphoria each time
↓
Process B intensifies → severe withdrawal pain, craving
↓
😰 Motivation shifts: pleasure-seeking → PAIN AVOIDANCE
↓
🔄 Addiction maintained by avoiding withdrawal, not seeking euphoria
