📖 Topic Overview

Biopsychosocial model of stress · Neurochemistry · Coping · Mediators

🧬

Biopsychosocial Model

Stress = personal evaluation of external events vs. coping resources. Results in physiological responses. Disparity between experience and expectations/resources determines eustress vs. distress.

⚗️

Neurochemical Axis

HPA Axis: Hypothalamus → CRH → Pituitary → ACTH → Adrenal → Cortisol. Sympathetic: Locus Ceruleus → Norepinephrine. Mood: Raphe Nucleus → Serotonin.

⚖️

Cognitive Appraisal

Primary appraisal: Is it eustress or distress? Secondary appraisal: Are my coping resources sufficient or insufficient? Together they dictate the physiological response.

🛡️

Stress Buffers

Direct (↓ physiological response) or Indirect (↑ cognitive appraisal). Includes Type A/B personality, locus of control, self-efficacy, hardiness, humor, social support, exercise, spirituality.

⏱️ Stress Response Stages (Internal Component)
Stage 1
Alarm Reaction
Body recognizes the stressor. Sympathetic NS activated. Fight-or-flight response initiated. Catecholamines surge.
Stage 2
Resistance
Body attempts to adapt and cope. Cortisol secretion sustained. HPA axis continuously active. Resources being depleted.
Stage 3
Exhaustion
Coping resources depleted. Maladaptation occurs: anxiety, depression, burnout, immune suppression, psychosomatic illness.
Outcome A
Eustress → Adaptation ✅
Successful coping (sufficient resources) → Effort without distress → High catecholamines, suppressed cortisol → Positive growth.
Outcome B
Distress → Deterioration ❌
Insufficient resources → Learned helplessness → Distress without effort → High cortisol, immune suppression, depression.

⚔️ Types of Psychological Conflict

🤩
Approach ↔ Approach
Two positive goals
Choosing between two equally desirable positive options. Both are good — that's the stress!

Example: Physician choosing between two prestigious fellowship programs in different countries.
😱
Avoidance ↔ Avoidance
Two negative outcomes
Forced to choose between two undesirable options. Trapped feeling.

Example: Must do volunteer work at a clinic she hates OR write a 20-page paper on a topic she hates.
😬
Approach ↔ Avoidance
Positive goal + negative consequence
One goal that is both attractive and unattractive. Want the goal but fear the cost.

Example: Wanting to become a surgeon (positive) but dreading years of sleep-deprived residency (negative).
🤯
Multiple App-Avoid
Multiple mixed alternatives
Several options each with both positive and negative consequences. Most complex type.

Example: Choosing between several job offers — each has good salary but bad location, or great team but poor pay.

💊 Management Strategies

Adaptive vs maladaptive · Arousal vs transaction management

⚠️ Key Distinction: Adaptive = healthy. Maladaptive = harmful. Arousal management targets physiology. Transaction management targets cognition/environment.
Arousal Management
🧘
Physiological Reduction
  • Medication (e.g., beta-blockers, anxiolytics)
  • Relaxation training (progressive muscle, breathing)
  • Biofeedback — real-time physiological control
  • Exercise — direct catecholamine/cortisol modulation
  • Meditation / mindfulness practices
Transaction Mgmt — Problem
🔧
Problem-Focused Coping
  • Direct action: remove or modify the stressor
  • Problem-solving: create a study schedule (e.g., scenario 1)
  • Seek information / learning new skills
  • Time management and goal restructuring
  • Used when the problem is controllable and changeable
Transaction Mgmt — Emotion
❤️
Emotion-Focused Coping
  • Cognitive redefinition (Taylor, 1983)
  • Reappraising the stress as tolerable or acceptable
  • Distraction and positive reinterpretation
  • Journaling / expressive writing
  • Used when the problem is uncontrollable or unchangeable
Stress Buffers — Direct
🛡️
Direct Buffers (↓ Physiology)
  • Regular exercise (cortisol reduction)
  • Humor → ↑ salivary IgA immune boost
  • Adequate sleep and nutrition
  • Spiritual and religious support
Stress Buffers — Indirect
🧠
Indirect Buffers (↑ Appraisal)
  • Build internal locus of control
  • Strengthen self-efficacy ("I can do it")
  • Social support (be careful of disclosure risks)
  • Cultivate Hardy personality (3 Cs)
  • Develop Type B behavioral traits
⛔ MALADAPTIVE
🚫
Maladaptive Strategies (Avoid)
  • Substance use / addiction (alcohol, drugs)
  • Aggression and hostility
  • Social withdrawal and isolation
  • Unhealthy defense mechanisms (denial, projection)
  • Avoidance behaviors and procrastination
